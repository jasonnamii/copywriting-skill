# Interface: Hit-Skill Consumption
## How copywriting-skill activates 3-layer hit patterns

---

## LAYER 1: 16 MECHANISM AXES → COPY STRATEGY

### Axis 1: 이중처리 (Dual Processing S1/S2)
**Copy Strategy:** Balance rapid recognition with considered trust. Use S1 triggers (pattern recognition, emotion) for initial engagement, S2 reasoning (evidence, specificity) for commitment.

**Benefits Most:** A1-A3 (onboarding), C3-C4 (landing/ads), D1 (retention)

**Copy Technique:** Two-tier messaging—headline/visual for S1, supporting detail for S2.
- KR: "직관적으로 쓰세요" (직관=S1) + "과학적으로 증명됐습니다" (증명=S2)
- EN: "Feels right instantly" (S1) + "because 73% of creators agree" (S2)

**Implementation:** Lead with emotion, follow with data. Never data-first.

---

### Axis 2: 손실회피 (Loss Aversion)
**Copy Strategy:** Frame benefit as prevention of loss, not gain. Loss is 2.5x more motivating than equivalent gain.

**Benefits Most:** B1-B2 (strategy docs), C6-C8 (email/nurture), D1-D2 (retention/upsell)

**Copy Technique:** "Don't miss," "Avoid," "Prevent," "Protect"—activate loss frame.
- KR: "지금 놓치면 다시 오지 않습니다" (손실강조)
- EN: "Miss this, and your competitors won't"

**Implementation:** Replace "gain X" with "avoid losing X." Test loss-frame variants.

---

### Axis 3: 휴리스틱 (Heuristics)
**Copy Strategy:** Provide mental shortcuts. Use availability (frequency), representativeness (type-matching), and anchoring (first number).

**Benefits Most:** A4-A5 (notifications), C2 (homepage), C4 (ads)

**Copy Technique:** Reference prevalence, use relatable archetypes, anchor with strong first number.
- KR: "1,000,000명의 크리에이터가" (가용성휴리스틱)
- EN: "Every creator does this" (representativeness)

**Implementation:** Lead with power numbers. Use "millions," "everyone," "your peers."

---

### Axis 4: 스토리텔링 (Storytelling/WYSIATI)
**Copy Strategy:** Embed concrete narrative—customer journey, founder origin, problem-solution-result. WYSIATI: What You See Is All There Is.

**Benefits Most:** C1 (brand), C5-C6 (social/email), D1 (retention)

**Copy Technique:** Problem→Complication→Turn→Resolution. Show protagonist (reader) overcoming obstacle.
- KR: "처음엔 어려웠지만, [해결책으로] 주간 매출이 3배 늘었어요"
- EN: "Sarah spent 3 hours weekly editing. Now, 5 minutes. Here's how."

**Implementation:** Every major copy block should have a 2-3 sentence story arc.

---

### Axis 5: 사회적증거 (Social Proof)
**Copy Strategy:** Leverage community validation. Types: expert endorsement, user testimonial, quantity signal, similarity signal.

**Benefits Most:** C2-C3 (homepage/landing), C4 (ads), D1-D2 (retention/upsell)

**Copy Technique:** Attribute credibility—"2,000+ creators," "CEO of TechCorp said," "Your peers use..."
- KR: "유명한 크리에이터들이 선택했습니다" (전문가) + "당신 같은 사람들이" (유사성)
- EN: "Featured in Forbes. 4.9★ from 5,000+ users."

**Implementation:** Vary proof types. Testimonials > quantity. Similarity > fame.

---

### Axis 6: 호혜 (Reciprocity)
**Copy Strategy:** Give value first (guide, template, audit, trial). Reader feels obligated to reciprocate (purchase, trial, share).

**Benefits Most:** B3-B5 (internal/support), C6-C8 (email nurture), D3 (lifecycle)

**Copy Technique:** Open with free resource—"Download our playbook," "Try free for 14 days," "See your competitor scores."
- KR: "무료 감사 리포트 받으세요" → (다음 이메일) "자세한 솔루션은..."
- EN: "Get your free brand audit. Then we'll show you what to fix."

**Implementation:** Reciprocity email sequence: Value → Scarcity → Offer. Min 3-email gap.

---

### Axis 7: 인지부하 (Cognitive Load)
**Copy Strategy:** Minimize friction. Shorter copy > longer. Lists > paragraphs. 1 CTA > 3 CTAs.

**Benefits Most:** A1-A2 (onboarding), A9 (loading), C4 (ads)

**Copy Technique:** Delete every non-essential word. 6-word limit per line. Scannable structure.
- KR: "좋아요 > 팔로워가 늘어요 > 수익이 되죠" (리스트)
- EN: "Sign up. Connect Instagram. Post. Done." (4 words max per line)

**Implementation:** Read copy aloud. If you pause mid-sentence, it's too long. Split.

---

### Axis 8: 내적동기 (Intrinsic Motivation/SDT)
**Copy Strategy:** Appeal to autonomy (choice), competence (mastery), relatedness (belonging). Avoid controlling language ("must," "have to").

**Benefits Most:** A1 (onboarding), C1 (brand), D1 (retention)

**Copy Technique:** Frame action as self-directed. "Discover," "Unlock," "Become" vs "You need to," "You should."
- KR: "당신의 스타일을 표현하세요" (자율성) > "이렇게 해야 합니다"
- EN: "Become a confident creator" (autonomy + competence) vs "Do what creators do"

**Implementation:** Count directive language. Replace "must/should" with "can/discover."

---

### Axis 9: 외적보상 (Extrinsic Rewards)
**Copy Strategy:** Use tangible incentives (free month, bonus, cash). But avoid undermining intrinsic motivation.

**Benefits Most:** A4-A6 (notifications/promo), C4 (ads), D2-D3 (upsell/winback)

**Copy Technique:** Make reward crystal clear and time-bound. "$100 credit," "Extra month free," "Bonus 5,000 followers."
- KR: "지금 가입하면 첫 달 50% 할인" (구체+시간제한)
- EN: "Upgrade today. Get 30 free credits + priority support."

**Implementation:** Reward must be > cost to acquire. Test 1:1.5 to 1:3 ratios.

---

### Axis 10: 동조 (Conformity)
**Copy Strategy:** Describe normative behavior. What's the default? Make "correct" behavior visible.

**Benefits Most:** C2 (homepage), C5 (social), D1-D2 (retention/upsell)

**Copy Technique:** "Creators are," "Everyone's," "The standard now," "Join the shift."
- KR: "모든 크리에이터는 이미 사용 중" vs "소수만 아는"
- EN: "This is how 80% of creators work now."

**Implementation:** Conformity is passive persuasion. Use sparingly, only when true.

---

### Axis 11: 정체성 (Identity)
**Copy Strategy:** Appeal to self-image. "For creators," "For founders," "For people who..."

**Benefits Most:** A1 (onboarding), C1 (brand), C5-C6 (social/email), D1 (retention)

**Copy Technique:** Use identity mirrors. "Like you, we believe," "Your type of creator," "For ambitious builders."
- KR: "크리에이터를 위해 만들었습니다" (정체성선언)
- EN: "Creators choose Notion." (Not "Notion is popular.")

**Implementation:** Who are they? Build copy around their identity, not your product.

---

### Axis 12: 지위 (Status)
**Copy Strategy:** Appeal to prestige, exclusivity, premium positioning. Scarcity + quality signal.

**Benefits Most:** A10 (premium onboarding), C1 (brand), C3 (landing), D1-D2 (retention/upsell)

**Copy Technique:** "Premium," "Exclusive," "Invited," "Elite," "Only for..." + high price signal.
- KR: "상위 1%의 크리에이터만" (지위신호)
- EN: "The creator's choice for brand protection."

**Implementation:** Status copy requires visual/design alignment. Design must feel premium.

---

### Axis 13: 진화적경계 (Evolutionary Vigilance)
**Copy Strategy:** Activate cautious attention. Urgency, scarcity, threat-detection. High-stakes framing.

**Benefits Most:** C3-C4 (landing/ads), C6 (email), D2 (upsell)

**Copy Technique:** Scarcity signals: "Limited to X," "Ends Thursday," "Only Y spots left." Threat: "Your competitors already..."
- KR: "이번 달만 가능합니다" (시간제한) / "경쟁사는 이미..."
- EN: "This offer disappears Friday" / "While spots last"

**Implementation:** Vigilance copy must be TRUE. False scarcity destroys trust.

---

### Axis 14: 짝짓기신호 (Mating Signals)
**Copy Strategy:** Appeal to status, health, resource-signaling. High-status affiliations, prestige indicators.

**Benefits Most:** A1 (premium onboarding), C1 (brand), C5-C6 (social/email), D1 (retention)

**Copy Technique:** Associate with high-status figures, brands, outcomes. "As used by," "Featured in," "For the ambitious."
- KR: "일론머스크도 하는 방식" (고지위인물) / "포브스 추천"
- EN: "What ambitious creators use" / "As featured in TechCrunch"

**Implementation:** Status signals must be credible. Fake endorsements kill credibility.

---

### Axis 15: 감정전염 (Emotional Contagion)
**Copy Strategy:** Transmit feeling through words. Use emotionally-charged language, concrete imagery, first-person testimony.

**Benefits Most:** C1 (brand), C5-C6 (social/email), C8 (storytelling), D1 (retention)

**Copy Technique:** Use sensory detail, emotional verbs, voice of authentic humans. Avoid corporate-speak.
- KR: "정말 짜릿했어요" (감정동사) vs "효과적입니다" (객관)
- EN: "I felt alive creating again" vs "The tool is effective"

**Implementation:** Include one real person's emotional quote per major section.

---

### Axis 16: 행동관성 (Behavioral Inertia)
**Copy Strategy:** Reduce friction to default behavior. Make new action easy, align with existing habit.

**Benefits Most:** A2-A3 (onboarding), A9 (loading), B4 (internal), D1 (retention)

**Copy Technique:** "Just," "One click," "In 30 seconds," "Like you already do." Habit-stacking language.
- KR: "Instagram처럼 쓰면 돼요" (기존습관과의 연결)
- EN: "If you can like a post, you can do this."

**Implementation:** Reduce steps. Compare to existing behavior. Show 30-second demo, not tutorial.

## LAYER 2: 6 STIMULUS DESIGN FORMULAS → COPY EXPRESSION

### Formula 1: Trigger → Motivation → Ability → Action
**Translation:** Problem statement → Desire reframe → Easiness proof → Simple CTA

**V-Checks Required:**
- V1: Problem is specific, felt urgently by audience
- V2: Reframe to achievable outcome exists
- V3: Proof of ease available (testimonial, demo, or principle)

**Template Structure:**
1. **Problem Line:** "You spend [time] on [task], watching it drain [resource]."
2. **Reframe Line:** "It doesn't have to be this way. [Audience type] who switched now [outcome] in [timeframe]."
3. **Proof Line:** "How? [Mechanism]. One customer: [quote]."
4. **CTA Line:** "[Simple action verb]. Try free for [timeframe]."

**Examples:**

KR Example 1:
```
문제: "매일 2시간 편집하느라 업로드가 밀려있죠?"
리프레임: "상위 크리에이터는 30분에 끝냅니다."
증명: "AI가 컷을 제안하니까요. 한 유저: '이젠 더 많이 올릴 시간이 생겼어요'"
CTA: "무료 체험. 30초. 지금."
```

EN Example 1:
```
Problem: "You edit 2 hours. Upload still waits."
Reframe: "Top creators finish in 30 minutes."
Proof: "AI suggests cuts. One user: 'Now I upload 3x more.'"
CTA: "Try free. 30 seconds. Now."
```

KR Example 2:
```
문제: "브랜드 톤을 일관되게 유지하기 힘들죠?"
리프레임: "큰 팀처럼 일관성 있게 할 수 있어요."
증명: "가이드를 한 번 설정하면 AI가 적용합니다. 팀: '편집자 회의를 안 해도 돼요'"
CTA: "브랜드 톤 가이드 5분만에 만들기. 시작."
```

EN Example 2:
```
Problem: "Brand voice slips across channels."
Reframe: "Maintain consistency like a 50-person team."
Proof: "Set tone once. AI enforces it. Team: 'No more editor meetings.'"
CTA: "Build your voice guide in 5 min. Start."
```

### Formula 2: Urgency × Scarcity × Social Proof
**Translation:** Time boundary + Resource limit + Social validation = Action now

**V-Checks Required:**
- V1: Scarcity is genuine and time-bound
- V2: Social proof is specific (count, quote, or source)
- V3: Consequence of waiting is clear

**Template Structure:**
1. **Scarcity Opener:** "[Spots/Supply/Offer] is limited to [X]"
2. **Social Proof Middle:** "[Y% / count] already have. Latest: '[Quote]'"
3. **Urgency Close:** "Expires [Day]. Join before [date]."

**Examples:**

KR Example 1:
```
"올해 베타 100명만 받습니다. 이미 80명이 가입했고, 한 명은 '주간 매출이 10배 됐어요'라고 했어요. 12월 31일까지만."
```

EN Example 1:
```
"Only 20 beta spots. 18 are claimed. One early member: 'Revenue jumped 10x.' Closes Dec 31."
```

KR Example 2:
```
"이번 달 특가는 50자리. 35자리가 찼고, 입장한 팀들의 평균 리뷰는 4.9/5. 금요일 자정 가격 올라갑니다."
```

EN Example 2:
```
"Early-bird pricing: 15 seats left. 35 teams claimed theirs. Rating: 4.9/5. Price increases Friday midnight."
```

### Formula 3: Before-After-Bridge (Transformation)
**Translation:** State A (present problem) → State B (desired future) → How bridge (product/solution)

**V-Checks Required:**
- V1: Before state is relatable, not generic
- V2: After state is visually/emotionally specific
- V3: Bridge mechanism is clear and credible

**Template Structure:**
1. **Before:** "You're [specific situation]. Stuck because [root cause]."
2. **After:** "Imagine [specific changed state]. Now you can [concrete outcome]."
3. **Bridge:** "Here's the move: [mechanism]. Takes [time]."

**Examples:**

KR Example 1:
```
이전: "동영상 편집에 밤을 새니까 창의력이 남아나지 않죠."
이후: "편집은 AI가 하고, 당신은 재미있는 아이디어에만 집중한다면?"
브릿지: "우리 템플릿을 골라서 클릭 3번. 5분이면 끝."
```

EN Example 1:
```
Before: "Night-after-night editing drains your creativity."
After: "AI edits. You ideate. Create 3x more, sleep better."
Bridge: "Pick template. Click 3 times. Done in 5 minutes."
```

KR Example 2:
```
이전: "매주 댓글에 답하는데 2시간이 걸려서 콘텐츠 기획을 못 해요."
이후: "댓글 답변은 자동으로 (당신 톤으로), 당신은 다음 영상을 구상하는 거죠."
브릿지: "톤을 학습시키는데 3분. 그 다음부턴 자동."
```

EN Example 2:
```
Before: "2 hours replying to comments means no content planning."
After: "Replies auto-generate (your voice). You focus on next viral idea."
Bridge: "Train our AI your tone. 3 minutes. Then automatic."
```

### Formula 4: Specificity × Credibility (The Credibility Ladder)
**Translation:** General claim → Specific metric → Evidence → Authority → Reader trust

**V-Checks Required:**
- V1: Metric is specific (% or count, not "better")
- V2: Evidence is attributed and recent (date, name, or publication)
- V3: Authority is relevant to audience

**Template Structure:**
1. **Claim:** "[Outcome] by [audience]"
2. **Metric:** "Average: [X% improvement or count]"
3. **Evidence:** "Based on [X] users / study / brand audit"
4. **Authority:** "As published by [credible source] / Verified by [credential]"

**Examples:**

KR Example 1:
```
주장: "크리에이터들이 더 빨리 성장합니다."
수치: "평균 팔로워 증가율은 23% 높습니다."
근거: "2,341명의 한국 크리에이터 3개월 추적 데이터"
권위: "Creator Economy Journal (2025년 1월)"
```

EN Example 1:
```
Claim: "Creators grow faster."
Metric: "Average follower growth increases 23%."
Evidence: "Analysis of 2,341 Korean creators, 3-month study"
Authority: "Creator Economy Journal, January 2025"
```

KR Example 2:
```
주장: "마케팅 비용을 줄입니다."
수치: "CAC(고객획득비용)는 평균 34% 감소"
근거: "B2B SaaS 87개 회사 8개월 벤치마크"
권위: "Sequoia Capital portfolio data"
```

EN Example 2:
```
Claim: "Cuts marketing spend."

Evidence: "87 B2B SaaS companies, 8-month benchmark"
Authority: "Sequoia Capital portfolio analysis"
```

### Formula 5: Objection Disarming (Pre-empt Doubt)
**Translation:** Anticipate hesitation → Acknowledge legitimate concern → Provide evidence → Reframe

**V-Checks Required:**
- V1: Objection is the #1 barrier for this copy type
- V2: Answer is evidence-backed (not deflection)
- V3: Reframe leads to action

**Template Structure:**
1. **Concern:** "You might be thinking: '[Common doubt]'"
2. **Acknowledge:** "That's fair. [Legitimacy statement]"
3. **Evidence:** "[Specific data/testimonial] shows [resolution]"
4. **Reframe:** "Actually, [new perspective that makes sense]"

**Examples:**

KR Example 1:
```
우려: "혹시 AI가 내 톤을 죽일까봐 걱정되죠?"
인정: "맞아요, 많은 크리에이터가 그래요."
증명: "근데 실제로는? 우리 유저 44%는 '톤이 더 좋아졌다'고 했어요. 왜냐면 AI는 제안할 뿐, 당신이 고르니까."
리프레임: "생각해보면, 좋은 도구는 당신을 대체하지 않고 증폭시킵니다."
```

EN Example 1:
```
Concern: "Won't AI kill my voice?"
Acknowledge: "Valid worry. Many creators have it."
Evidence: "In reality? 44% of users said 'My voice got better.' Why? AI suggests, you choose."
Reframe: "Tools don't replace good writers. They amplify them."
```

KR Example 2:
```
우려: "비싸지 않을까?"
인정: "네, 가격이 중요하죠."
증명: "평균 고객은 첫 달에 추가 수익으로 비용을 거뜬히 회수합니다 (데이터: 평균 ROI 320% Y1)"
리프레임: "비용이 아니라 투자. 그리고 가장 싼 투자."
```

EN Example 2:
```
Concern: "Isn't it expensive?"
Acknowledge: "Price matters. I get it."
Evidence: "Average customer breaks even on first-month revenue gain. (ROI: 320% Y1)"
Reframe: "It's not a cost. It's the highest-ROI investment you'll make."
```

### Formula 6: Momentum (Step-by-step credibility stacking)
**Translation:** Small wins → Visible progress → Pattern recognition → Large commitment

**V-Checks Required:**
- V1: First step is micro-commitment (free, 30-sec, no email)
- V2: Each step shows progress (visual, count, or milestone)
- V3: Large ask comes after trust is built

**Template Structure:**
1. **Micro Step:** "Start with [tiny, free action]. Takes [<2 min]."
2. **Progress:** "You'll see [immediate, visible result]."
3. **Momentum:** "Most people then [natural next step]."
4. **Close:** "[Big ask] is the obvious next move."

**Examples:**

KR Example 1:
```
1단계: "채널 URL 하나만 붙여넣으세요. 10초 끝."
결과: "실시간으로 경쟁사 분석이 나타나요."
동기: "대부분 여기서 '어? 저희가 이렇게 밀려있었나' 깨닫고"
큰 요청: "전사 감사 리포트($2,000)를 받으러 옵니다."
```

EN Example 1:
```
Step 1: "Paste one channel URL. 10 seconds."
Result: "Real-time competitive analysis appears."
Momentum: "Most people realize they're behind and want the full audit."
Big ask: "Then they invest in the enterprise report."
```

KR Example 2:
```
1단계: "간단한 3문제 퀴즈 풀기. 2분."
결과: "당신의 브랜드 톤 유형 (6가지 중 하나)이 나와요."
동기: "유형을 보니 '오, 나랑 딱 맞는 템플릿이 있네' 싶고"
큰 요청: "템플릿 구독($29/월)으로 매달 톤 가이드 업데이트 받기."
```

EN Example 2:
```
Step 1: "Take 3-question brand quiz. 2 minutes."
Result: "Discover your tone type (one of 6)."
Momentum: "See templates designed for your type, get curious."
Big ask: "Subscribe to monthly tone guides ($29/mo)."
```

## LAYER 3: 5 PROPAGATION AXES → COPY DISTRIBUTION

### Axis 1: Narrative Propagation (Story momentum)
**How propagation influences copy structure:**
- Copy must stand alone AND work in social excerpt
- First 40 chars are headline (for shares)
- Twist/surprise at sentence 2-3 drives reshare
- Ending is call-to-forward ("Share this with...?"), not just CTA

**Propagation-critical types:** C5 (social), C6 (email), C8 (storytelling), B2 (playbooks)

**Shareability features:**
- Counterintuitive claim (surprises are shared 34% more)
- Emotional specificity (not generic)
- "Us vs. them" or "then vs. now" framing
- Closing question that invites response

**C8 Mode Pattern:**
Creator community responds to raw, unpolished truth. Copy should feel:
- Conversational (contractions, fragments)
- Specific to creator context (mention struggles creators face)
- Humble (admit what doesn't work)
- Actionable (one takeaway per post)

**Copy format for C8 mode:**
```
HOOK (emotion or surprise):
"I spent $50k on influencer marketing. Zero ROI."

BODY (raw detail):
"Turns out, creators want [specific value], not [common misunderstanding]."

TWIST (reframe):
"When I switched to [approach], 40% became customers."

FORWARD:
"What's your biggest creator marketing mistake? Reply below."
```

---

### Axis 2: Structural Propagation (Copy density for viral spread)
**How propagation structure influences copy structure:**
- Shorter copy spreads further (< 140 words)
- Bullet points + whitespace > paragraphs
- One big claim + proof + CTA (3-block structure)
- Hashtag integration (not spam, but strategic)

**Propagation-critical types:** C4 (ads), C5 (social), D3 (lifecycle/winback)

**Shareability features:**
- Scannable (2-3 sec read)
- Proof is visual-ready (statistic or image-friendly)
- CTA is social-forward ("Share your version," "Tag someone who...")
- Hashtag = movement participation (not product push)

**C8 Mode Pattern:**
Minimal copy + trendy format = high resonance.
- Format: Text + visual + TikTok/Reel audio cue
- Copy constraint: Max 4 lines on visual
- Tone: Conversational, emoji-appropriate (sparingly)

---

### Axis 3: Social Network Propagation (Type of sharing)
**How propagation structure influences copy strategy:**
- Private share (WhatsApp, email) = longer, more evidence-based
- Public share (Twitter, LinkedIn) = shorter, more provocative
- Network size bias = @mention high-status people (early propagation booster)
- Weak ties carry news faster = non-obvious audiences often reshare most

**Propagation-critical types:** C5 (social), C6 (email), C8 (thought leadership)

**Shareability features:**
- Private: Full evidence, no hype, "I thought of you because..."
- Public: Hot take with proof, high-status nod ("As [famous person] said..."), question to network
- Weak-tie: Translate jargon (make insider-only ideas accessible)

**C8 Mode Pattern:**
Creators share with audience first (not peers). Copy strategy:
- Teach something useful (not hype)
- Credit sources/ideas (builds trust network)
- Invite participation ("Drop your approach in replies")
- Make audience the hero, not product

---

### Axis 4: Temporal Propagation (Timing and sequencing)
**How propagation structure influences copy cadence:**
- Day 1: Surprise/hook (short, disruptive)
- Day 2-3: Evidence/depth (medium, persuasive)
- Day 4-7: Proof/community (testimonials, numbers)
- Week 2: Urgency/offer (scarcity frame)
- Week 3: Objection disarm (hesitant prospects)

**Propagation-critical types:** C6 (email), C7 (nurture), D1-D3 (retention/upsell/winback)

**Shareability features:**
- Stagger information (curiosity gap = share-inducing)
- Early pieces are hook + teaser (standalone shareable)
- Later pieces assume audience = loyal (can be longer, more detailed)
- Re-share triggers every 3-4 days (best time for network amplification)

**C8 Mode Pattern:**
Creator audiences have short attention. Sequence:
- Monday: Problem/pain point (relatable post, high engagement)
- Wednesday: Behind-the-scenes (how creator solved it, authentic)
- Friday: Blueprint/framework (actionable tool, shareable)
- Sunday: Community win (user result, trust-building)

---

### Axis 5: Cultural Propagation (Values alignment and movement)
**How propagation structure influences copy values:**
- Align with audience's cause, not your product category
- Anti-pattern matters (what we're against = identity signal)
- Movement-making copy (part of larger cultural shift)
- Community language (insider vocab, shared beliefs)

**Propagation-critical types:** C1 (brand), C5 (social), C8 (thought leadership), B1-B2 (strategy)

**Shareability features:**
- Take a stand (polarize slightly to unite tribe)
- Insider language (use community vocabulary, not corporate)
- David-vs-Goliath framing (underdog = share-worthy)
- Invite membership ("Join the [movement]," not "Buy [product]")

**C8 Mode Pattern:**
Creators are movements, not just users. Copy should:
- Celebrate creator identity (not just product features)
- Acknowledge creator struggles (real barriers, not sanitized)
- Invite creator leadership ("Be the first in your niche to...")
- Make product a vehicle for creator movement, not the movement itself

## ACTIVATION PROTOCOL

When copywriting-skill receives a brief, run this sequence:

### Step 1: L1 Screening (Which axes are relevant?)
**Input:** Copy brief (type, audience, goal)
**Process:**
1. Identify audience's top 3 pain axes (loss-aversion? cognitive load? status?)
2. Identify distribution context (email = long; social = short; internal = neutral)
3. List applicable axes (typically 3-5 of 16)

**Output:** Axis scorecard
```
Brief: C4 Ad copy for creators (performance focus)
Axes: 
- Axis 2 (Loss Aversion): HIGH [missed growth opportunity]
- Axis 13 (Vigilance): MEDIUM [competitors moving faster]
- Axis 8 (Intrinsic Motivation): HIGH [autonomy/mastery appeal]
- Axis 5 (Social Proof): HIGH [community validation]
- Axis 7 (Cognitive Load): MEDIUM [quick proof]
```

### Step 2: L2 Formula Selection (Which expression techniques?)
**Input:** Axis scorecard + copy type requirements
**Process:**
1. Match top 3 axes to formulas (usually Formula 1-3 are bread-and-butter)
2. Check V-conditions (Can we prove this? Is it relevant?)
3. Layer formulas (Momentum often works well as final layer)

**Output:** Formula brief
```
Primary: Formula 1 (Trigger→Motivation→Ability→Action)
- Problem: "Slow growth despite consistent posting"
- Desire: "Consistent 20%+ monthly growth"
- Ability: "AI suggests optimal posting time+format"
- CTA: "See your growth potential in 30 seconds"

Secondary: Formula 2 (Urgency×Scarcity×Proof)
- Insert before CTA: "[X creators] already see [Y%] faster growth"
```

### Step 3: L3 Propagation Design (If applicable)
**Input:** Copy type + distribution channel + audience (e.g., C8 creators on TikTok)
**Process:**
1. Identify propagation axis (narrative, structural, social network, temporal, cultural)
2. Apply C8 mode patterns if audience is creator-centric
3. Design shareability hooks (question, controversial take, or "tag someone")

**Output:** Propagation brief
```
Type: C5 (social) + C8 mode
Axis: Narrative + Cultural
Pattern: Counterintuitive claim → creator-specific proof → forward question
Hook: "Why the best creators secretly *aren't* trying to go viral"
Shareability: End with "What's your anti-growth strategy? 👇"
```

### Step 4: Synthesis → Copy Brief
**Combine all layers into final copy brief:**

```
COPYWRITING BRIEF
Type: C4 (Ad copy)
Axes Active: Loss aversion, Social proof, Intrinsic motivation
Primary Formula: Trigger→Motivation→Ability→Action
Secondary Formula: Urgency×Scarcity×Proof
Propagation: Narrative (creator-centric) + Structural (scannable)
C8 Mode: Yes (audience = creators on Instagram/TikTok)

Tone: Conversational, specific, actionable
Length: Headline (< 10 words), body (< 40 words), CTA (< 5 words)
Proof points: Specific metric + creator testimonial
Shareability: End with question inviting creator participation

Headline options (test 3):
1. Problem-hook: "Why your best posts get 10% engagement"
2. Counterintuitive: "Growth happens when you stop chasing it"
3. Proof-lead: "Your competitors are posting 2x/day. Here's why."
```
