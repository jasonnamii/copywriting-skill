# Universal Mode: Standard Tone Guide

The reliable, clear, trustworthy baseline. This is the voice when creators need to feel in control—when clarity beats cleverness. Benchmarks: 토스, Notion, Slack, Stripe.

---

## Standard Tone DNA

### Core Principles
- **Clarity above all.** If someone wakes up at 3 AM confused, you've failed.
- **Trust through precision.** Specific numbers, clear next steps, no shortcuts.
- **Warmth without personality overload.** You're helpful, not trying to be their friend.
- **Predictability.** Language shouldn't surprise; it should reassure.
- **Accessibility.** A beginner should understand. An expert shouldn't feel talked-down-to.

### Sentence Structure
- **Active voice, short sentences.** "You control your data" not "Data is controlled by users."
- **One idea per sentence.** If you need a semicolon, split it.
- **Avoid hedging.** Not "might help" but "helps" or "may help only if..."
- **Be specific about scope.** "This works with Figma files under 50MB" not "Generally compatible."

### Word Choice
- **Common words.** Use "help" not "facilitate," "send" not "transmit."
- **Avoid marketing-speak.** "Faster" not "optimized," "easier" not "streamlined."
- **Numbers are truth.** "19 bucks a month" beats "affordable pricing."
- **Action verbs.** "See your data," "download files," "invite teammates."

### Tone Markers
- Honest about limitations
- Respectful of user time
- Transparent about trade-offs
- Confident without overselling

### What Standard is NOT
- Cold or robotic
- Overly formal ("shall," "hereby," "pursuant")
- Self-deprecating or false modesty
- Bland to the point of useless
- Patronizing or oversimplified

## Usage Notes

- **Universal** applies to all types (A, B, C, D)
- **Standard tone is the default** when hip/experimental don't apply
- Examples are product-ready
- Korean uses natural, accessible language (not formal keigo)
- English uses American English, accessible to non-native speakers
- Anti-patterns show what clarity loses
- B types (B1-B5) are **strategy docs only** — appear in internal communications, investor decks, company blogs
