# Product Copy: Hook Model + Positioning + Bly's 4S Quality Framework

## PART 1: Hook Model (Eyal - Habit-Forming Products)

**Framework Definition:** Products become habit-forming through a 4-stage loop: Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment. Copy for each stage drives the loop.

### Stage 1: Trigger (External → Internal)

**Definition:** What prompts user action? External triggers are copy prompts (emails, CTAs, notifications). Internal triggers are emotional connections (anxiety, boredom, desire).

**Product Copy Technique:** Link your product to frequent external triggers, then create internal triggers through habit-forming copy.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Name the moment when users will need you (morning, during meetings, when stuck).
- Create emotional trigger language: "When you're blocked..."
- Use time-based references tied to daily rhythm.
- Connect product to existing habits (morning coffee, email checking).

**Examples (A-Types Only):**
- Email subject: "Your code is blocked. Help arrived." (Habit/workflow trigger) 🌐 Universal
- Hero headline: "When you hit a bug at 11pm, here's your lifeline." (Moment-based trigger) 🌐 Universal
- 한국 예제: "월요일 아침 당신의 첫 일은 어떤가요? 우리가 그걸 5분으로 줄였습니다." (Routine trigger) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A1 (Hero/trigger moment), A2 (Primary/habit context)

---

### Stage 2: Action (The Easiest Path to Reward)

**Definition:** What action does the user take to get the reward? Copy here removes friction and makes action irresistible.

**Product Copy Technique:** Show that the action is simpler than they expect. Remove choice. Use micro-copy.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Make the action visible and single: "Click here to see your result."
- Use specific micro-language: "Paste your code" not "Input your data."
- Reduce decision points to one.
- Use progressive disclosure: show only what's needed now.

**Examples (A-Types):**
- CTA: "Paste your code. We'll analyze it in 30 seconds." (Specific action, clear outcome) 🌐 Universal
- Next step: "See which part is slow." (Single, obvious action) 🌐 Universal
- 한국 예제: "이 코드를 여기에 붙여넣으세요. 2초면 됩니다." (Specific, fast) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A1 (CTA), A2-A3 (Primary/secondary action prompts)

---

### Stage 3: Variable Reward (Why They Keep Using It)

**Definition:** Unpredictable rewards are more habit-forming than predictable ones. Copy creates desire for the reward and makes the reward feel slightly unpredictable.

**Product Copy Technique:** Position the reward as surprising, valuable, and somewhat unpredictable. Create anticipation.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Show what's possible but don't reveal everything upfront.
- Use language of discovery: "See what we found," "Uncover," "Discover."
- Emphasize that the reward is personalized/unique to them.
- Reference the variety of insights they'll get.

**Examples (A-Types):**
- "We'll show you 5 things that surprise you about your code." (Specific number, element of surprise) 🌐 Universal
- "See insights only your team will find." (Personalized, exclusive) 🌐 Universal
- 한국 예제: "당신의 코드에서만 나올 수 있는 병목을 찾아낼 거예요." (Personalized discovery) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A3-A4 (Secondary/detail - what they'll discover)

---

### Stage 4: Investment (Making Them Stick)

**Definition:** User invests effort (data, attention, customization) which increases likelihood they'll return. Copy here creates commitment and personal investment.

**Product Copy Technique:** Ask for small investments that create commitment. Make the investment feel valuable to them.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Request small commitments: review, save, customize.
- Connect investment to personal stake: "Save your preferences," "Rate this insight."
- Explain why investment matters (improves future results).
- Make the payoff from investment clear.

**Examples (A-Types):**
- "Save your code snapshot. You'll compare it monthly." (Investment → future benefit) 🌐 Universal
- "Tell us your goal (speed or quality). We'll optimize for what matters to you." (Investment → personalization) 🌐 Universal
- 한국 예제: "당신 팀의 코드 스타일을 한번 등록하면, 다음부턴 더 정확해집니다." (Investment → improvement) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A4 (Detail/settings), A5 (Onboarding/setup)

---

## Hook Model Copy Playbook for A-Types

**A1 (Hero/Headline):** Lead with Trigger (when they'll use it) + Action (how easy)
- "When you're debugging, paste your code. We'll find the bug."

**A2 (Primary):** Action (how easy) + Variable Reward (what's possible)
- "Upload your repo in 60 seconds. See your performance gaps immediately."

**A3 (Secondary):** Variable Reward (what insights) + why it matters
- "Every analysis shows 3-5 things you can fix today. Some take 5 minutes."

**A4 (Detail):** Investment requests + payoff
- "Connect your CI/CD pipeline. We'll track improvements weekly. You'll see the trend."

**A5+ (Narrative/Onboarding):** Full hook loop in story form
- "Developer hits a slow endpoint (Trigger) → Runs analysis (Action) → Finds memory leak (Variable Reward) → Saves it to dashboard (Investment) → Checks dashboard every Friday to verify optimizations hold (Habit)"

## PART 2: Positioning (Ries & Trout)

**Framework Definition:** Position is what's in the customer's mind about your product. Own a unique position first, then defend it through copy.

### Core Positioning Rules

**Rule 1: Own One Thing in Their Mind**

**Copy Application:** Choose one unique claim and reinforce it relentlessly. Ignore what competitors claim unless directly relevant to your differentiation.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Lead with your single, ownable claim in every communication.
- Use the same language repeatedly (brand language consistency).
- When talking features, tie them back to the core position.
- Avoid claiming two things equally; pick the primary.

**Examples:**
- Position: "The code review tool that teaches."
- Every headline reinforces this: "Reviews that level up your team," "Code review as education," "Learn while you review."
- 🌐 Universal

- Position: "The fastest way to ship alone or with a team."
- All copy points back to speed + flexibility:
- 🎨 C8

**Rule 2: Match the Customer's Existing Category**

**Definition:** Don't invent a new category unless you can own it. Piggyback on existing mental categories and reposition within them.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Name what category you're in: "The code review tool that..." (category = code review tool)
- Then add the unique twist: "...that teaches"
- Use category language first, then differentiation.
- Avoid saying "It's like nothing you've seen before" unless you own the new category.

**Examples:**
- "It's a code review tool. But unlike others, it learns from each review." (Category + differentiation) 🌐 Universal
- "팀 협업 도구인데, 지루한 문서 공유 대신 대화 중심입니다." (Category + twist) 🌐 Universal
- "A no-code builder. But for APIs, not interfaces." (Category + specificity) 🎨 C8

**Rule 3: Occupy "Better/Faster/Cheaper" but Pick One**

**Definition:** Customers remember brands by one superiority claim. Pick your hill: quality, speed, or price.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Make ONE of these your claim: better quality, faster speed, lower cost.
- Don't try all three (customers won't believe it).
- Structure copy to prove your claimed superiority.
- Make competitors' superiority in other areas irrelevant to your buyer.

**Examples:**
- **Quality positioning:** "We trade speed for correctness. Your reviews catch every issue." 🌐 Universal
- **Speed positioning:** "Deploy in half the time. We've optimized for speed." 🌐 Universal
- **Price positioning:** "Same features as [competitor] at 1/3 the price. We've cut the cruft." 🌐 Universal

**Rule 4: Resist Line Extension**

**Definition:** Copy temptation to add new features/positions dilutes your core position. Defend it ruthlessly.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- When launching new features, don't make new claims.
- Tie new features back to core position: "This teaches your team even better."
- In copy, defend your territory; don't expand.
- If you must expand, create a sub-brand, don't dilute main claim.

**Examples:**
- Temptation: "Now with security scanning, API testing, and monitoring!" ❌
- Better: "These features exist to serve our core: helping you review code that teaches." ✓
- 🌐 Universal

**Rule 5: Long-Term Consistency in Copy**

**Definition:** Positioning works only if you own it for years. Copy consistency across time builds mental ownership.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Use the same headline structure across campaigns.
- Reference your position regularly, not as secondary claim.
- Don't abandon your position to chase trends.
- Evolve the expression, not the core claim.

**Examples:**
- 2024: "Code review that teaches"
- 2025: "Reviews that grow your team"
- 2026: "Learning through every review"
(Same position, evolved language)
🌐 Universal

## PART 3: Bly's 4S Framework (Universal QC Checklist)

**Framework Definition:** Every piece of copy should satisfy these four criteria. Use this as final QC before shipping any copy type.

### S1: Specificity

**Definition:** Vague claims die unread. Specific claims stick and feel credible.

**QC Questions:**
- Are there numbers? (40% faster, not "faster")
- Are there names/specific examples? (Sarah, a designer, not "users")
- Are there dates/timeframes? (30 days, 5 minutes, not "soon")
- Are there specific actions described? (paste, upload, click not "get started")

**Application (All Copy Types):**
- Replace "significant savings" with "saves 8 hours/week"
- Replace "many customers love it" with "5,000+ developers ship faster"
- Replace "improves your workflow" with "cuts setup time from 2 hours to 10 minutes"

**Examples:**
- ❌ Vague: "This tool is great for teams."
- ✓ Specific: "When your team uses this, code reviews drop from 3 hours to 45 minutes." 🌐 Universal

- ❌ Vague: "당신의 생산성이 높아질 것입니다."
- ✓ Specific: "당신은 3시간의 설정 시간을 40분으로 줄일 겁니다." 🌐 Universal

- ❌ Vague: "Creators love our tool."
- ✓ Specific: "600 creators shipped their first course this month using our template." 🎨 C8

---

### S2: Simplicity

**Definition:** Short words, short sentences, short copy. Remove everything that doesn't serve the core message.

**QC Questions:**
- Are sentences under 20 words? (If not, split them)
- Are words under 3 syllables? (If not, replace or justify)
- Is each paragraph one idea? (If it covers two ideas, split)
- Would a 12-year-old understand the core message? (If not, simplify)

**Application (All Copy Types):**
- Replace "facilitate accelerated code review workflows" with "ship faster code reviews"
- Replace "utilizing advanced algorithms" with "using our analysis"
- Replace "consequent improvements in team cohesion" with "your team learns from each review"

**Examples:**
- ❌ Complex: "Our sophisticated analysis paradigm leverages machine learning to optimize development velocity."
- ✓ Simple: "We analyze your code. You get faster." 🌐 Universal

- ❌ Complex: "우리의 획기적 접근 방식은 조직적 신시너지를 극대화합니다."
- ✓ Simple: "우리가 당신의 일을 정리합니다. 당신은 일에만 집중합니다." 🌐 Universal

---

### S3: Sincerity

**Definition:** Truth builds trust. Exaggeration kills it. Every claim must be defensible and honest.

**QC Questions:**
- Can I prove this claim with data/evidence?
- Am I using absolute language (always, best, only) when hedging would be more honest?
- Have I used hyperbole? (Replace with specific truth)
- Does the copy feel like a pitch or a conversation? (Conversation = higher sincerity)

**Application (All Copy Types):**
- Replace "revolutionary" with "saves 8 hours/week"
- Replace "the best" with "top-rated by users in Y category"
- Replace "guaranteed to change your life" with "8/10 users report significant improvement"

**Examples:**
- ❌ Insincere: "This will transform your entire business."
- ✓ Sincere: "60% of teams report faster deployments within 30 days." 🌐 Universal

- ❌ Insincere: "당신의 삶이 완전히 바뀔 것입니다."
- ✓ Sincere: "당신은 월요일을 좋아하게 될 겁니다." (Specific transformation, more believable) 🌐 Universal

---

### S4: Significance

**Definition:** Why does this matter? Copy must connect to customer values, not just features.

**QC Questions:**
- Does this solve a problem the customer actually has? (If explaining features without problem, missing significance)
- Does this appeal to a core human value? (Freedom, mastery, belonging, safety, status)
- Would the customer share this with a peer? (If not, low significance)
- Does it connect to the bigger vision, not just the product?

**Application (All Copy Types):**
- Add significance to features: "5-minute setup" → "You own your time again"
- Add significance to benefits: "Faster reviews" → "Your team learns and ships"
- Add significance to outcomes: "Better code" → "You're proud of what you ship"

**Examples:**
- ❌ Low significance: "Our tool has 10 integrations."
- ✓ Significant: "Stop switching between 10 tools. All your code work happens in one place." 🌐 Universal

- ❌ Low significance: "우리는 클라우드 기반입니다."
- ✓ Significant: "어디서나, 언제나, 당신의 일을 할 수 있습니다." 🌐 Universal

- ❌ Low significance: "Faster exports."
- ✓ Significant: "Ship your course in 2 hours instead of 2 days. Reach your audience sooner." 🎨 C8

## Bly's 4S Applied to Each Copy Type

### A1 (Hero/Headline)
- ✓ Specific number or claim → Headline
- ✓ Simplicity → Headline is under 10 words
- ✓ Sincerity → Claim is provable
- ✓ Significance → Speaks to core value (time, status, learning, freedom)

**Example:** "Deploy code 3x faster. Ship with confidence." (Specific, simple, sincere, significant) 🌐 Universal

### A2 (Primary)
- ✓ Specificity → Concrete outcome with number
- ✓ Simplicity → 1-2 sentence benefit
- ✓ Sincerity → Backed by social proof or data
- ✓ Significance → Ties to outcome that matters (team growth, personal mastery)

**Example:** "5,000 engineers use this for code reviews. They ship 40% faster and catch 2x more issues." 🌐 Universal

### A3 (Secondary)
- ✓ Specificity → Name a specific use case or moment
- ✓ Simplicity → List 3 concrete benefits max
- ✓ Sincerity → Use customer quotes if possible
- ✓ Significance → Show transformation, not feature

**Example:** "당신의 팀이 리뷰 회의 시간을 1시간에서 15분으로 줄일 겁니다." (Specific, simple, shows transformation) 🌐 Universal

### A4 (Detail/FAQ)
- ✓ Specificity → Technical specs, numbers, comparisons
- ✓ Simplicity → Answer in 1-2 sentences per question
- ✓ Sincerity → Show limitations alongside strengths
- ✓ Significance → Frame features in terms of outcomes

**Example:** "Q: How long does setup take? A: 10 minutes. You'll analyze your first repo in 12." 🌐 Universal

### A5-A7 (Narrative/Case Study)
- ✓ Specificity → Named customer, specific numbers, timeline
- ✓ Simplicity → Story arc: problem → action → result
- ✓ Sincerity → Direct quotes from customer
- ✓ Significance → Transformation is the story

**Example:** "Sarah's team shipped 50% faster in 6 weeks. 'I thought we had a process problem. It was a tool problem.' Here's her story." 🌐 Universal

### B1-B5 (Universal/Brand)
- ✓ Specificity → Proof points with numbers where possible
- ✓ Simplicity → Core claim in 10 words or fewer
- ✓ Sincerity → Third-party validation
- ✓ Significance → Why the brand matters philosophically

**Example:** "We help 50K teams ship code they're proud of." (Specific number, simple, third-party proof implied, significant) 🌐 Universal

### C1-C4 (Creator Product)
- ✓ Specificity → Time saved, work samples, before/after
- ✓ Simplicity → Casual, conversational language
- ✓ Sincerity → Acknowledge the problem honestly
- ✓ Significance → Tie to creative vision, not just convenience

**Example (C8 mode):** "Spend 2 hours on design. Let the tool handle 20 hours of export/format hell." (Specific time, casual, significant) 🎨 C8

### D1 (Awareness)
- ✓ Specificity → Surprising fact or stat
- ✓ Simplicity → One insight per message
- ✓ Sincerity → Research-backed
- ✓ Significance → Why it matters to their life

**Example:** "Your code reviews are killing your team's learning. (We measured 47 teams. Here's what we found.)" 🌐 Universal

### D2 (Engagement)
- ✓ Specificity → Concrete offer, clear value
- ✓ Simplicity → Single CTA
- ✓ Sincerity → No pressure, clear safety net
- ✓ Significance → Why now matters

**Example:** "See how your code compares. Free analysis, no credit card, results in 60 seconds." 🌐 Universal

### D3 (Conversion)
- ✓ Specificity → Guarantee, timeline, proof points
- ✓ Simplicity → Clear next step
- ✓ Sincerity → Address the last objection
- ✓ Significance → Show the transformation they're buying

**Example:** "Join 5K teams shipping faster. 30-day money-back guarantee. Start free, no setup needed." 🌐 Universal

## Product Copy Playbook: Combining Hook Model + Positioning + 4S

### The Complete Product Copy System

**Step 1: Establish Your Position (Rule: Own One Thing)**
- Define: What will be in customer's mind about us? (e.g., "The code review tool that teaches")
- Verify: Is this unique + believable + defensible?
- Test: Can we prove it in copy?

**Step 2: Map the Hook Model to Customer Journey**
- Trigger: What moment/emotion brings them to us?
- Action: How do we make the first action obvious?
- Variable Reward: What insight/surprise do they get?
- Investment: What commitment deepens their habit?

**Step 3: Write Each Copy Type Through Hook Loop**
- A1 (Hero): Trigger + Action (how easy)
- A2 (Primary): Action + Variable Reward
- A3 (Secondary): Variable Reward + Investment
- A4 (Detail): Investment payoff + proof
- A5+ (Narrative): Full loop in story form

**Step 4: QC Every Piece with 4S Checklist**

| Copy Element | Specificity | Simplicity | Sincerity | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Headline | Number or specific claim | <10 words | Provable | Core value |
| Primary benefit | Concrete outcome + number | 1-2 sentences | Social proof or data | Transformation |
| Secondary benefit | Specific moment/use case | 3 benefits max | Customer quote | Emotional payoff |
| CTA | Specific action + timeframe | Clear next step | No pressure, safety net | Why now |
| Proof | Named customer, specific #s | <2 sentences | Direct quote | Real outcome |

**Step 5: Test Position Consistency**
- Does every headline reinforce the core position?
- Do we own this claim or are we borrowing from competitors?
- Would a customer repeat our position to a peer?
- Have we defended this position across 6 months of copy?

## Position Framework for Different Product Categories

**Developer Tools:**
- Position: Quality (code reviews that teach), Speed (deploy in half the time), or Cost (enterprise features at indie price)
- Hook loop: Moment of pain (stuck bug) → simple action (paste code) → surprising insight (performance leak found) → investment (save result, track weekly)

**Creator Tools (A/C-Types):**
- Position: Time (ship 10x faster), Creative freedom (no boring UI), or Community (connect with other creators)
- Hook loop: Inspiration moment → quick first creation (5 min) → share-worthy result → audience connection
- 🎨 C8 tone: Casual, empowering, anti-corporate

**Enterprise/B-Types:**
- Position: Risk mitigation, Security, or ROI
- Hook loop: Trust-building moment → pilot project → measurable improvement → company-wide adoption

## Common Positioning Mistakes (Watch Out)

1. **Trying to own two things** → Customers remember one. Pick it.
2. **Following competitors' claims** → Own your own territory, not theirs.
3. **Changing position every quarter** → Consistency builds mental ownership. Stick 2+ years.
4. **Claiming "best" without proof** → Use specific numbers: "40% faster," "top-rated by..."
5. **Positioning for competitors, not customers** → Write to solve customer problems, not to one-up competitors.

## Quick Position + Hook Audit Checklist

Before shipping copy:

- [ ] Can I state our position in one sentence? If it takes two, I haven't narrowed enough.
- [ ] Does this trigger moment matter to our core customer?
- [ ] Is the first action (A1 CTA) obvious and friction-free?
- [ ] Does the variable reward (A3 benefit) feel slightly surprising?
- [ ] Does the investment (A4 ask) feel reasonable given the reward?
- [ ] Can I pass the 4S test: Specific numbers? Simple language? Sincere claims? Significant outcome?
- [ ] Did I avoid hyperbole? (Test: replace "revolutionary" with specific benefit)
- [ ] Does every piece of copy reinforce the same position?

**If you answer no to more than 2, revise before shipping.**
