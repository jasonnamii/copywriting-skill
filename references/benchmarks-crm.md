# Benchmarks — CRM/Retention Copy

> Mode tags: 🎨 C8 mode | 🌐 Universal | 🔄 Both

## D1 Retention / Re-engagement Email

### Superhuman — Inbox Zero achievement email
- **Copy (subject):** "[Your name], you've achieved Inbox Zero 🎉"
- **Copy (body):** "Remember when email felt overwhelming? You just cleared your last email. This moment—Inbox Zero—is what Superhuman users live for. Keyboard shortcuts: you've saved 3 hours this week alone. Next power move: set up your custom snippets to save another 2 hours daily."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Congratulations (achievement celebration); mirrors user's own goal; social proof embedded (what Superhuman users live for); metrics prove value (3 hours saved); forward momentum (next power move) keeps engagement loop alive.

### Figma — Design system adoption re-engagement
- **Copy (subject):** "Your team just adopted 45 components from Figma's design system"
- **Copy (body):** "Great news: your team's component adoption jumped 45% this month. That means faster designs, fewer inconsistencies, and more time for creative thinking instead of pixel-pushing. Teams that hit 40%+ adoption see 30% faster handoff times."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Celebrates progress with specific number (45%); reframes technical metric as creative benefit ("more time for creative thinking"); peer benchmark (40%+ adoption teams) motivates further action; benefits are concrete, not abstract.

### Airbnb — "Your next adventure awaits" re-engagement
- **Copy (subject):** "We found 3 homes you love in [City you browsed]"
- **Copy (body):** "[Name], you've been eyeing [City]. We found 3 places that match your taste: cozy studio in [Neighborhood], rooftop with a view, and beachside bungalow. Prices dropped 15% for these dates. Your perfect stay is waiting."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🔄
- **Why it works:** Personalization (browsing history + taste detection); scarcity (price drop); curated (3 matches, not 300); benefit-driven ("perfect stay"); action is frictionless (pre-filtered).

## D2 Customer Success / Onboarding Email Series

### Intercom — Customer success email template
- **Copy (email 2 of 5 - Feature discovery):**
- **Subject:** "Here's a feature that'll save your team 5 hours every week"
- **Body:** "Hi [Name], a lot of users overlook our Broadcast feature. But teams using it spend 80% less time on repetitive outreach. One customer, [Company], saved 4 hours/week. Want the quick demo? [Video link] Takes 3 minutes."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🌐
- **Why it works:** Benefit up-front (save 5 hours); social proof (Company used it, 80% less time); low friction (3-minute demo); optional engagement (want the demo = permission-based); specific to use case (repetitive outreach).

### Loom — Creator onboarding progression
- **Copy (email 3 of 6 - Advanced): "Your first 5 videos hit 2,000 views. Here's what creators do next"**
- **Body:** "You've shared 5 videos. They got 2,000 combined views. That's a signal people want to watch your content. Next step: creators usually add captions (40% more engagement) and link to a landing page. Here's how both work together: [guide link]"
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Anchors to user's specific behavior (5 videos, 2K views = they're real); reframes as progress signal (people want your content); expert pattern ("creators usually..."); specific next action with measured benefit (captions = 40% engagement).

### 토스 (Toss) — Financial onboarding progression (reconstructed)
- **Copy (email 2 of 4 - Investment education): "의료비 걱정? 이렇게 모아두세요"** (Worried about medical expenses? Here's how to save for them)
- **Body:** "토스 사용자 77%가 손쉬운 투자로 '의료자금 따로 모아두기'를 해요. 한 달에 5만원부터 시작할 수 있어요. 당신도 해볼까요? [투자 바로 시작] 다른 토스 투자자들이 뭘 하고 있는지 확인해보세요."
- **Language:** KR
- **Mode:** 🌐
- **Why it works:** Addresses actual need (medical fund anxiety); social proof (77% of Toss users do this); low-entry friction (50K won = about $40); casual tone (당신도 해볼까요 = "why not you?"); peer discovery (see what others do) = normalization.

## D3 Review Solicitation / NPS / Feedback Loop

### Zappos — Post-delivery survey
- **Copy (email subject):** "How was your Zappos experience? (Quick 2-minute survey)"
- **Copy (body):** "We just shipped your order. Before it arrives, we'd love your feedback: Did our service feel human? Fast? Easy? Your honest answer helps us stay Zappos. [Survey link] Thanks for being awesome."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🌐
- **Why it works:** Timing (pre-arrival = while experience is fresh); low-friction promise (2 minutes); values alignment ("feel human, fast, easy" = brand pillars); permission-based ("honest answer"); gratitude personalized (You = Zappos customer).

### Linear — Feature feedback request
- **Copy (in-app modal):** "You just used our 'Cycles' feature. What should we improve about it?" [Optional open text]
- **Copy (follow-up email if skipped):** "We noticed you're using Cycles but haven't shared feedback. Two quick questions: (1) Is it helping you plan better? (2) What's missing? Your answer helps 100K+ Linear users."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Captures feedback at moment of use (highest signal); contextual (about Cycles, not general); low barrier (optional, open-ended); follow-up creates social obligation ("helps 100K users") without pressure; scales individual feedback to community benefit.

### 오늘의집 (Today's House) — Review solicitation (reconstructed)
- **Copy (email subject):** "[사용자], 집 만드는 여정 공유해주세요"** ([User], share your home-making journey)
- **Copy (body):** "당신이 공유한 인테리어 게시물이 5,000명에게 봤어요. 당신의 다음 프로젝트는? 집꾸미기 후기를 남겨주면 다른 사람들도 용감하게 시작할 수 있어요. [리뷰 쓰기]"
- **Language:** KR
- **Mode:** 🔄
- **Why it works:** Celebration of prior contribution (5K views); community framing (helps others be brave); reframes review as narrative ("journey"), not transactional rating; peer motivation (다음 프로젝트) keeps momentum; Korean communal values (도움 = helping neighbors).

---

**D-Type Pattern Summary:**

### D1 (Re-engagement)
- Celebrate progress with data-backed metrics
- Reframe technical achievements as creative or human benefits
- Show what's next (forward momentum keeps loop alive)
- Personalization + scarcity + curation = irresistible offer

### D2 (Customer Success / Onboarding progressions)
- Anchored to user's specific behavior (not generic)
- Social proof + expert patterns ("creators usually...")
- One clear next action with measured benefit
- Permission-based (would you like, want to try, ever considered)

### D3 (Review/NPS/Feedback)
- Capture at moment of use or recent memory (not weeks later)
- Low-friction promise (2 minutes, optional, open-ended)
- Reframe feedback as contribution to community
- Social obligation (helps others, validated by crowd) vs. direct ask

---

**Mode notes:**
- 🎨 **C8 mode** examples emphasize creator identity, power-user respect, minimal friction, proprietary terminology
- 🌐 **Universal** examples prioritize clarity, trust-building, specific metrics, permission-based engagement
- 🔄 **Both** examples work across audiences through community framing and cultural values
