# Benchmarks — Marketing/External Copy

> Mode tags: 🎨 C8 mode | 🌐 Universal | 🔄 Both

## C1 Campaign Landing Pages (Hero Copy)

### Apple — Think Different campaign (1997)
- **Copy:** "Here's to the crazy ones. The misfits. The rebels. The troublemakers. The round pegs in square holes. The ones who see things differently... Because the people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world, are the ones who do."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🌐
- **Why it works:** Brand-centric, not product-centric; emotional/philosophical framing; uses archetype (rebels, misfits) to invite self-identification; narrative arc (problem → vision → resolution).

### Stripe — "The payments infrastructure for the internet"
- **Copy:** "Stripe makes it easy to start, run, and scale an online business. Millions of companies rely on Stripe to accept payments, send payouts, and manage their business operations online."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🌐
- **Why it works:** Job-to-be-done framing (start/run/scale); social proof embedded (millions); multi-benefit stacking; B2B trust language (rely on).

### Linear — "The system for product development"
- **Copy:** "Linear is the best place to plan, track, and ship work. Designed for high-performing teams."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Aspirational positioning ("best place"); verb stack (plan/track/ship) = narrative flow; "high-performing" appeals to elite identity; understated confidence.

## C2 Product Feature Pages

### Vercel — "Build faster with Vercel"
- **Copy:** "Ship faster with code-to-web previews and interactive commenting. Deploy instantly and collaborate in real-time across your entire team."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Verb-first (Ship/Deploy); concurrent benefit stacking (faster + collaborative); implied competition (vs. traditional workflow); team-centered framing.

### Figma — Multiplayer design
- **Copy:** "Work together in real-time on a single file. Leave comments, build together, and ship faster than ever."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Real-time = technology as enabler; "on a single file" = clarity; cascade of benefits (comments → build → ship); shipping = end-game validation.

### Notion — All-in-one workspace
- **Copy:** "One workspace. Every tool you need. Docs, databases, wikis, and more—all in one place."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🔄
- **Why it works:** Antidote framing ("one" vs. tool fragmentation); capability list implies ecosystem completeness; "all in one place" = mental simplification.

## C3 Performance/Product Ads

### 배달의민족 (Baedal Minjok) — Brand campaign
- **Copy:** "배달의민족 안 쓴 사람이 있나요?" (Is there anyone who doesn't use Baedal Minjok?)
- **Language:** KR
- **Mode:** 🌐
- **Why it works:** Rhetorical question triggers social proof (universal adoption); casual tone ("안 쓴 사람") = mass-market language; assumes ubiquity as proof.

### Nike — Performance positioning
- **Copy:** "Just Do It." [in context of athlete imagery]
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🌐
- **Why it works:** Imperative + simplicity = action mandate; culturally iconic; verb-only (no product mention) = philosophy not product.

### Spotify — "Your daily soundtrack"
- **Copy:** "Discover, listen to, and share music with Spotify."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🔄
- **Why it works:** Lifestyle framing (soundtrack); verb stack (discover/listen/share) = engagement loop; ownership ("your") = personalization promise.

## C4 Email Campaign Sequences

### Superhuman — Onboarding email series
- **Copy:** "We'll help you become superhuman at email. Your first session shows you the way." [followed by video tutorials with benefit callouts]
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Aspirational identity ("superhuman"); guided narrative (we'll help); first action is low-friction (watch); series expects multiple touchpoints = habit formation.

### Airbnb — Re-engagement campaign (reconstructed)
- **Copy:** "[Name], we found something special for you in [City]" / "A place like no other. Your perfect stay awaits."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🔄
- **Why it works:** Personalization + discovery framing; "like no other" = aspirational curation; "perfect stay" = outcome promise; emotional (awaits = anticipation).

### 오늘의집 (Today's House) — Design inspiration email
- **Copy:** "오늘 공유된 따뜻한 집들을 구경해보세요." (Check out the warm homes shared today.)
- **Language:** KR
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Emotional anchoring (따뜻한 = warm, not just "nice"); time-sensitive (오늘) = scarcity; invitation tone ("구경해보세요") = discovery, not hard sell.

## C5 SNS / Social Media Copy

### Duolingo — Twitter/X campaign
- **Copy:** "The owl is watching. Practice your lesson or face consequences." [with meme-style imagery]
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Brand character activation (owl as entity); gamified threat (watching); ironic tone appeals to Gen Z; on-platform language (meme format).

### 무신사 (Musinsa) — Instagram Stories campaign
- **Copy:** "이 계절의 핫템 ⚡ 당신의 스타일은?" (This season's hot items. What's your style?)
- **Language:** KR
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Trend positioning (핫템); cultural emoji (⚡ = lightning = energy); question format = engagement hook; "당신의" = inclusivity.

### Linear — LinkedIn campaign
- **Copy:** "We ship faster than you change your Slack status." [with performance benchmarks]
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Competitive positioning through humor; specific comparison (Slack = shared context); LinkedIn B2B audience appreciates edge; metric credibility.

## C10 PR / Press Release Copy

### Arc Browser — Product announcement
- **Copy:** "Arc is the browser that puts you in control. With Boosts, Notes, and Spaces, Arc transforms how you use the internet—from chaotic tabs to intentional browsing."
- **Language:** EN
- **Mode:** 🎨
- **Why it works:** Control narrative = agency; proprietary terminology (Boosts, Notes, Spaces) = differentiation; problem diagnosis (chaotic tabs); outcome-focused (intentional).

### 당근마켓 (Carrot Market) — Expansion announcement
- **Copy:** "당근마켓은 이웃을 만나고, 신뢰를 나누는 플랫폼입니다. 우리는 따뜻한 동네 문화를 만들고 있습니다." (Carrot Market is where you meet neighbors and share trust. We're building warm neighborhood culture.)
- **Language:** KR
- **Mode:** 🌐
- **Why it works:** Mission-first (not transaction); emotional anchoring (따뜻한 = warmth); community values (이웃 = neighbor, 신뢰 = trust); culture narrative (not just transactions).

---

**Collection notes:**
- C8 mode excels in irreverence, proprietary language, cultural fluency (memes, trends), and creative identity
- Universal mode relies on clarity, social proof, benefit stacking, and trust markers
- Korean campaigns lean heavily into emotional/cultural values (warmth, taste, discovery) vs. technical differentiation
