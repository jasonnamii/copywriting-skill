# Persuasion Frameworks: Cialdini's 6 Principles + StoryBrand SB7 + Ogilvy Principles

## PART 1: Cialdini's 6 Principles of Influence

### Principle 1: Reciprocity

**Definition:** People are obligated to return favors. Give value first, and people naturally want to reciprocate.

**Copy Technique:** Give away genuine value before asking for a purchase. Make the gift valuable and specific.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Lead with a useful tip, resource, or exclusive access.
- Make the freebie valuable enough that the ask feels like natural reciprocation.
- Label it as a gift: "We're sharing this because..."
- Don't make reciprocation coercive; let obligation feel natural.

**Examples:**
- "Free audit: We'll analyze your code for performance leaks. No strings." (Useful gift, then natural upgrade path) 🌐 Universal
- "우리 팀이 정리한 초보자 가이드 (무료 pdf). 좀 더 깊게 배우고 싶으면 여기." (Free guide, then course offer) 🌐 Universal
- "We analyzed 10K creators' first months. Here's what works. (You can also hire us to do yours.)" (Free insight, then premium service) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A3-A4 (Educational + detail), B3-B5 (Thought leadership), D1-D2 (Awareness/engagement)

---

### Principle 2: Commitment & Consistency

**Definition:** Once people commit to something small, they want to be consistent with that commitment and escalate it.

**Copy Technique:** Start with a tiny commitment (quiz, checklist, micro-action). Then the larger ask feels consistent.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Begin with a small ask: "Take this 30-second quiz," "Check this box if..."
- Make the progression logical and small-step.
- Once committed, the bigger ask feels like continuation, not conversion.
- Use language that reinforces their commitment: "You checked box X, so..."

**Examples:**
- "Answer 3 questions about your workflow. (Then we'll show you how we'd optimize it.)" (Small quiz → bigger commitment) 🌐 Universal
- "이 5개 중 3개 이상 맞췄나요? 그럼 당신은 우리의 타겟 고객입니다." (Quiz commitment → sales message) 🌐 Universal
- "You said you want to ship faster. Here's the one tool that does that." (Referencing their stated goal creates consistency) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A2-A3 (Primary/secondary), B2-B4 (Engagement-focused), D2-D3 (Engagement/conversion)

---

### Principle 3: Social Proof

**Definition:** People believe things more when they see others doing/believing them. Especially powerful from peers.

**Copy Technique:** Show that similar people are already using it. Specific numbers and peer language are most credible.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Use peer-to-peer proof ("Used by 8K developers like you") not just testimonials.
- Show numbers of users + their type. "50K creators" > "many people."
- Quote users directly; their words are more convincing than your paraphrase.
- Show usage breadth across different contexts to maximize relatability.

**Examples:**
- "5,000 engineering teams use this for code review. Average adoption: 80% of team within 2 weeks." (Specific proof, specific context) 🌐 Universal
- "한국 스타트업 700개 사용 중. 같은 산업의 경쟁사도 4곳 사용 중." (Peer context + competitive proof) 🌐 Universal
- "On Product Hunt: 1,200 creators voted for this. Makers called it 'essential.'" (High-signal proof, peer language) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A2-A3 (Primary/secondary), A4 (Detail/credibility), B2-B3 (Social proof-heavy), C2-C3 (Creator adoption proof)

---

### Principle 4: Authority

**Definition:** People trust experts and official sources more than regular voices.

**Copy Technique:** Establish your credibility through credentials, methodology, or transparent expertise.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Show your methodology transparently. People respect "Here's how we tested."
- Use third-party validation over self-praise.
- Cite your credentials briefly, not at length.
- Show your work. Transparency about sources/data builds authority.

**Examples:**
- "Built by ex-Google performance engineers. We've helped teams optimize 50M+ lines of code." (Credentials + proof) 🌐 Universal
- "우리 팀은 한국 최대 5개 스타트업의 기술 고문입니다. 그 경험을 담았습니다." (Authority through past work) 🌐 Universal
- "Recommended by 47 published researchers. Used by MIT, Stanford, CMU." (Academic authority) 🎨 C8 & 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A2 (Primary), A4 (Detail/credibility), B1-B2 (Brand authority)

---

### Principle 5: Liking

**Definition:** People are persuaded by people they like. Factors: similarity, attractiveness, compliments, cooperation.

**Copy Technique:** Write like your audience, not at them. Find genuine common ground. Be warm, not salesy.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Use their language and reference their world without condescension.
- Find legitimate common ground: "We also hate [thing they hate]."
- Be genuine. Forced likeability feels manipulative.
- Show you enjoy working with customers (e.g., customer testimonials, team photos, casual tone).

**Examples:**
- "We hate config files too. That's why everything has smart defaults." (Common enemy, warmth) 🌐 Universal
- "우리도 당신처럼 야근에 지쳤습니다. 그래서 만들었습니다." (Shared frustration, empathy) 🌐 Universal
- "Creators told us they wanted a tool that doesn't feel like a 'tool.' We listened." (Respectful of customer perspective) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A5-A7 (Narrative-heavy), C1 (Hero/brand), D1-D2 (Brand awareness/engagement)

---

### Principle 6: Scarcity

**Definition:** People value things more when they're scarce or hard to get.

**Copy Technique:** Create legitimate scarcity (limited spots, deadline, exclusivity). Avoid fake scarcity; it kills trust.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Use scarcity only when genuine: limited beta spots, price increase deadline, limited supply.
- Be specific about the scarcity. "Only 20 spots left" > "limited spots."
- Pair scarcity with value: "Only 20 spots. Here's why it's exclusive." (Philosophy, not just limit)
- Use scarcity to create urgency, not desperation. Tone matters.

**Examples:**
- "We're accepting 30 customers this quarter to ensure personal onboarding. 8 spots remain." (Legitimate, specific, valuable scarcity) 🌐 Universal
- "가격이 내주 올라갑니다. 지금 가입한 150명은 평생 할인가를 유지합니다." (Real deadline, clear benefit to early adopters) 🌐 Universal
- "The free tier closes next month. Current users keep free access forever." (Scarcity + fairness) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A1 (Hero urgency), D3 (Conversion/closing), A2 (Primary action driver)

## PART 2: StoryBrand SB7 Framework

**Framework Definition:** A story structure that positions the customer as the hero and your brand as the guide.

### Element 1: Character (Customer is the Hero)

**Definition:** The customer, not your brand, is the protagonist. Your copy should be about *their* journey.

**Copy Template:** "Many [target customer] feel [internal struggle]. You're not alone. You've probably tried [what they've tried]. But [obstacle] keeps you stuck."

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Name the customer's frustration (internal struggle).
- Validate that their struggle is common and understandable.
- Show you understand their world specifically, not generically.

**Examples:**
- "Many developers feel frustrated by slow code reviews. You're not alone. You've probably tried longer review cycles or automation. But your team's learning gets lost in the process." 🌐 Universal
- "많은 창업자들은 회의에 지쳐있습니다. 당신도 그렇습니다. 이미 여러 도구를 써봤을 겁니다. 하지만 팀이 산만해집니다." (Character identification) 🌐 Universal
- "Creators feel torn between shipping fast and maintaining quality. You've felt this tension. You've tried frameworks and checklists. But they slow you down, not speed you up." (Internal struggle identification) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A5-A7 (Narrative-heavy), C1 (Hero), D1-D2 (Awareness)

---

### Element 2: Problem (External + Internal + Philosophical)

**Definition:** Three layers of problem. External: what they face. Internal: how it makes them feel. Philosophical: why it matters.

**Copy Template:** 
- External: "[Specific obstacle] keeps you from [goal]."
- Internal: "It makes you feel [emotion: doubt, frustration, overwhelm]."
- Philosophical: "And if this continues, [consequence]."

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Stack the layers. Don't just name the external problem; show how it *feels* and why it *matters*.
- Make the philosophical layer resonate with their deeper values.
- Use specific emotions tied to the problem.

**Examples:**
- External: "Slow code reviews block your deployment schedule."
- Internal: "It makes you feel like your team isn't moving fast enough."
- Philosophical: "And if this continues, you'll lose your best engineers to faster competitors."
🌐 Universal

**Korean Example:**
- External: "매일 밤 8시까지 일해야 합니다."
- Internal: "당신은 자책감을 느낍니다. 당신 시스템이 약하다고."
- Philosophical: "계속 이러면 가족과의 시간을 잃습니다."
🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A2-A3 (Primary/secondary), A5-A7 (Narrative), D2 (Engagement)

---

### Element 3: Guide (Brand as Wise Mentor)

**Definition:** Your brand enters as a trusted guide, not a salesman. You've been through this before; you know the path.

**Copy Template:** "That's why we [action]. We've learned [lesson] from working with [customers like them]. Here's what we know: [insight that shows you get it]."

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Show empathy first, then capability.
- Reference your experience with customers like them.
- Don't brag; share insight that proves you understand.
- Position yourself as a tool for *their* success, not your revenue.

**Examples:**
- "That's why we built a review platform that preserves learning. We've worked with 5K teams and learned: fast shipping means nothing if your team doesn't grow. Here's what we know: the best reviews teach." 🌐 Universal
- "그래서 우리는 야근을 없애는 시스템을 만들었습니다. 700개 팀과 일하며 알았습니다: 자동화는 시간을 줄이지만, 자동화 설계는 시간을 낭비합니다. 우리가 그 설계를 다 했습니다." 🌐 Universal
- "We've been there. 3K creators told us what blocks them. The answer wasn't more features—it was one tool that actually fits how you work." (Guide empathy + insight) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A4 (Detail), B1-B2 (Brand positioning)

---

### Element 4: Plan (Specific Steps to Success)

**Definition:** Give a clear, step-by-step plan for how the customer gets from problem to solution.

**Copy Template:** "Here's how we get you there: [Step 1]. Then [Step 2]. Finally [Step 3]. After [timeframe], you'll [outcome]."

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Keep it to 3-5 steps maximum.
- Make steps small and achievable.
- Reference the customer's goal at each step (not your process).
- Include a timeline for expectation-setting.

**Examples:**
- "Here's how you get there: (1) Connect your code repository. (2) We analyze your review patterns. (3) We suggest a review SLA that keeps quality high but ships fast. After 2 weeks, your deployment time drops 40%." 🌐 Universal
- "세 가지만 하면 됩니다: (1) 당신의 일과를 30초로 설명합니다. (2) 우리가 자동화할 부분을 제안합니다. (3) 자동화를 설정합니다. 1주일 후, 당신은 2시간을 돌려받습니다." 🌐 Universal
- "Our process is simple: (1) Upload your content. (2) Choose your format. (3) Publish everywhere. 15 minutes from start to finish." (Simple, outcome-focused) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A4 (Detail/how-to), A2-A3 (Primary/secondary), D3 (Conversion)

---

### Element 5: Challenge (CTA as Threshold Moment)

**Definition:** The CTA is a moment of decision—will they take action or stay stuck?

**Copy Template:** "If you're ready to [transformation], here's your first step: [specific action]. It takes [time/effort]. Then we'll [immediate benefit]."

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Make the CTA feel like a threshold, not a transaction.
- Be honest about effort required.
- Promise an immediate next step, not the whole solution.
- Use language of decision and commitment, not sales.

**Examples:**
- "If you're ready to ship code your team actually understands, here's your first step: Start a free trial. It takes 5 minutes. Then you'll see your first optimization suggestion." 🌐 Universal
- "당신이 야근을 끝내려면, 지금 신청하세요. 5분이면 됩니다. 그러면 우리가 당신의 일과를 분석한 보고서를 보낼 겁니다." 🌐 Universal
- "Ready to own your time? Start here. No credit card. One email. Then we'll show you exactly what you can automate." (Low-commitment threshold) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A1 (Hero CTA), D3 (Conversion CTA)

---

### Element 6: Transformation (Outcome)

**Definition:** Paint the picture of life *after* they've successfully used your product.

**Copy Template:** "When [timeframe], you'll [specific outcome]. You'll feel [emotion]. And you'll be able to [new possibility that was blocked before]."

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Make it specific and measurable.
- Include the emotional transformation, not just the practical one.
- Show the second-order benefit (what becomes possible now).
- Use vivid, present-tense language.

**Examples:**
- "After 30 days, you'll ship 40% faster. You'll feel confident your code is reviewed for understanding, not just syntax. And you'll have time to mentor junior engineers again." 🌐 Universal
- "3주 후, 당신은 6시에 퇴근합니다. 당신은 자책감 없이 웃습니다. 그리고 자녀와 저녁식사를 할 수 있습니다." (Practical + emotional + relational outcome) 🌐 Universal
- "You'll be the one bringing new ideas to the table. No more reactive firefighting. You'll feel like an owner again." (Identity transformation) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A5-A7 (Narrative), C1 (Hero), D2-D3 (Engagement/conversion)

---

### Element 7: Failure (What If They Don't Act)

**Definition:** Show the consequence of inaction. Make staying stuck feel worse than trying.

**Copy Template:** "If you don't [take action], you'll stay [stuck emotion/situation]. And a year from now, you'll [consequence]."

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Be honest, not manipulative. Real consequences, not fear-mongering.
- Connect to the philosophical problem from earlier.
- Make inaction feel like a choice they'll regret.
- Use this sparingly; it's powerful but can backfire if overused.

**Examples:**
- "If you don't improve your review process, your team stays burned out. And in 12 months, you'll have lost half your best engineers to competitors who got this right." 🌐 Universal
- "지금 행동하지 않으면, 내년 지금쯤 당신은 같은 야근을 하고 있을 겁니다. 아무것도 바뀌지 않습니다." 🌐 Universal
- "Skip this, and you're back to 10-hour days. Your competitors? They're shipping twice as fast." (Competitive urgency) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** D3 (Conversion/closing), A1-A2 (Urgency elements)

## PART 3: Ogilvy Principles (Modern Application)

### Principle 1: "The Consumer is Not a Moron"

**Definition:** Respect your audience's intelligence. Give them real information, not condescension.

**Modern Copy Application:** Use data, transparency, and complexity when warranted. Don't oversimplify just to be accessible. Audiences are smart.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Include detailed specs, research findings, or methodology if relevant.
- Avoid patronizing tone or oversimplified language.
- Respect that smart people like things simple—clarity is not insult.
- Trust the reader to understand nuance.

**Examples:**
- "Our algorithm combines latency optimization (reducing server response time) with intelligent caching (storing computed results). Here's the technical deep-dive." (Respects technical knowledge) 🌐 Universal
- "우리 알고리즘은 버틀러 검색 + 트리플 트리 구조를 씁니다. 기술 논문을 링크했습니다." (Respects intelligence, offers depth) 🎨 C8
- "Why we don't do X even though competitors do: [technical/philosophical reason]." (Transparency respect) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A4 (Technical detail), B3-B5 (Thought leadership), C2-C4 (Creator/technical tools)

---

### Principle 2: "The Advertisement Should Convey Information"

**Definition:** Copy should teach, not just persuade. The best ads are useful.

**Modern Copy Application:** Your copy should answer questions or provide insight people want. Information is persuasion.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Lead with useful facts or insights, not selling.
- Structure copy as education + application.
- Show your methodology or research.
- Make the copy itself worth reading.

**Examples:**
- "Your loading time costs you 7% of conversions per 100ms delay. Here's how to test yours. (Our tool helps with that.)" (Useful info → product relevance) 🌐 Universal
- "코드 리뷰가 느린 이유는 피드백이 아니라 승인입니다. 데이터를 보니 평균 대기시간이 3시간입니다. 여기 해결법이 있습니다." (Education-first, then solution) 🌐 Universal
- "Most creators spend 30% of their time on formats, not content. We analyzed 5K workflows. Here's what the efficient ones do differently." (Research → insight → product) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A3-A4 (Educational/detail), B3-B5 (Thought leadership), D1-D2 (Awareness/engagement)

---

### Principle 3: "Avoid Obvious Exaggerations"

**Definition:** Credibility dies with hyperbole. Understate if anything; let the product speak.

**Modern Copy Application:** Make claims you can back up. The most persuasive copy uses specific, modest claims.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Use "we" language about improvements, not "the best."
- Be specific, not superlative. "40% faster" beats "the fastest."
- Avoid: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "industry-leading" without proof.
- Let numbers and customer quotes do the claiming for you.

**Examples:**
- "40% faster deployments for 8K teams." (Specific, modest, proven) vs. "THE FASTEST DEPLOYMENT TOOL EVER" 🌐 Universal
- "우리 고객의 60%가 첫 달에 월급 인상을 받습니다." (Specific, modest) vs. "모두가 부자가 됩니다." 🌐 Universal
- "Saves you 6 hours a week on average." (Specific average) not "Saves up to 50 hours" (overstate with edge case) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A2-A4 (Primary/secondary/detail), B2-B3 (Credibility-focused)

---

### Principle 4: "Your Illustrations Should Ideally Show the Product in Use"

**Definition:** Show people how they'll use the product, not just the product itself.

**Modern Copy Application (Text Version):** Describe usage, not features. Paint the scene of the product in action.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Use scenario language: "When you [usage context], you can [outcome]."
- Show the workflow, not the interface.
- Describe the moment of transformation or insight.
- Use the customer's verb tense, not the product's.

**Examples:**
- "When you hit a bug at 11pm, instead of 'Let me search the code,' you say 'I found it.' 30 seconds." (Usage scenario) 🌐 Universal
- "당신이 미팅에 앉아있을 때, 휴대폰을 꺼낼 필요 없습니다. 모두가 이미 같은 페이지를 봅니다." (Usage context) 🌐 Universal
- "Your first publish happens at 3pm. It goes live in 3 frameworks. By 6pm, you're watching people use it." (Workflow scenario) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A5-A7 (Narrative), A2-A3 (Primary/secondary with usage), C1-C4 (Product in use)

---

### Principle 5: "Everybody Reads Headlines, But Only Half Read Body Copy"

**Definition:** Headline must be compelling enough to demand reading, but also informative enough to work standalone.

**Modern Copy Application:** Headline carries the full argument if body isn't read. Make both layers matter.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Headline should answer "Why should I care?" not just grab attention.
- Subheading should add one key piece of information (the mechanism or number).
- Every section heading should be a mini-argument, not just a label.
- Make scannable with strong subheadings.

**Examples:**
- Headline: "Deploy code in half the time."
- Subheading: "While shipping with more confidence."
(Headline carries main claim; subheading adds the emotional layer)
🌐 Universal

- Main headline: "당신의 팀이 더 빠르게 일합니다"
- Secondary: "아무도 번아웃되지 않으면서"
(Dual benefit structure)
🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A1 (Hero/headline focus), All A-types (headline hierarchy)

---

### Principle 6: "Make Your Advertisement Simple"

**Definition:** One idea per piece. One headline per page. Remove everything that doesn't serve the core message.

**Modern Copy Application:** Ruthless clarity. Cut anything that dilutes focus.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- One primary idea per section.
- One CTA per page (or one primary, one secondary).
- Remove competing claims.
- Use whitespace and section breaks to simplify scanning.

**Examples:**
- Single focus: "You ship faster" (not "You ship faster, with more confidence, and your team learns, and you save money") 🌐 Universal
- 한 가지만: "당신은 야근을 안 합니다" (not "당신은 야근을 안 하고, 팀이 성장하고, 창의력이 늘어납니다") 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** All copy types, especially A1 (Hero), D1-D3 (Campaign)

---

### Principle 7: "Use Research to Build Better Copy"

**Definition:** Don't guess. Let customer research, data, and testing guide every claim and tone.

**Modern Copy Application:** Reference your research. Show you know your audience better than you know yourself.

**Copy Application Rules:**
- Cite your research when relevant: "We surveyed 1K customers..."
- Use customer language (from interviews/surveys) in your copy.
- Test assumptions. Validate before publishing.
- Let data inform tone, structure, and claims.

**Examples:**
- "78% of developers said slow reviews frustrate them most. (We've fixed that specific pain.)" (Research-backed claim) 🌐 Universal
- "고객 인터뷰로 나온 가장 흔한 불만: 설정에 시간이 걸린다. 우리는 그것을 없앴습니다." (Research-driven product claim) 🌐 Universal
- "Our research showed creators want one tool, not five. That's what we built." (Research-driven positioning) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A2-A4 (Product positioning/detail), B1-B5 (All universal copy)

| **Authority** | Establishing credibility, high-stakes decisions | A2, A4, B1-B2 | Expert, transparent | Credentials + methodology |
| **Liking** | Building brand affinity, emotional connection | A5-A7, C1, D1-D2 | Warm, conversational | Common ground + empathy |
| **Scarcity** | Creating urgency, prioritizing action | A1, D3 | Honest, urgent | Real limit + reason |

## Framework Selection by Audience State

**Unaware/Problem-Aware Audience:**
- Use: StoryBrand (Character + Problem + Guide)
- Add: Ogilvy Information principle
- Avoid: Scarcity (they're not ready to commit)

**Solution-Aware Audience:**
- Use: Cialdini's Authority + Social Proof
- Add: StoryBrand Plan + Ogilvy Understatement
- Add: Comparison/differentiation (implicit authority)

**Product-Aware Audience:**
- Use: All Cialdini 6 Principles
- Add: StoryBrand Challenge + Failure
- Add: Scarcity (if legitimate)

**Most-Aware/Decision-Making Audience:**
- Use: Scarcity, Challenge, Commitment/Consistency
- Minimize: Narrative (they need clarity, not story)
- Add: Authority (third-party proof)
