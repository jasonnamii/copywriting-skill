# Sticky & Shareable Copy: SUCCESs + STEPPS Frameworks

## PART 1: SUCCES (Made to Stick)

### Simple

**Definition:** Strip away jargon and unnecessary complexity. Communicate the core idea in language your audience already knows, using short words and familiar concepts.

**Copy Application Rule:** Lead with your most essential message first. Use subject-verb-object sentence structures. Eliminate buzzwords. Every adjective must earn its place. Test: Can a 10-year-old understand your headline?

**Examples:**
- "We save you 10 hours per week." (Simple) vs. "Our platform optimizes temporal resource allocation efficiency." (Complex) 🌐 Universal
- "더 빨리, 더 싸게" (Faster, cheaper) vs. "성능 극대화 및 비용 최소화" (Performance maximization and cost minimization) 🌐 Universal
- Email subject: "Your refund is ready" not "Transactional credit reversal processed" 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A1 (Hero/Headline), A2 (Primary), B1-B5 (All universal copy), C1 (Hero), D1 (Primary message)

---

### Unexpected

**Definition:** Violate expectations in a controlled way. Break patterns your audience has learned to ignore. The surprise creates attention and makes the message stick.

**Copy Application Rule:** Start with the common belief, then contradict it with surprising data or claim. Use questions that make readers pause. Create cognitive dissonance, then resolve it. Avoid shock for shock's sake—surprise must serve your message.

**Examples:**
- "The best marketing is not a magic formula. It's just caring." (Contradicts the complexity myth) 🌐 Universal
- "9/10 dentists lied to you. The real truth is..." (Sets expectation, breaks it) 🎨 C8 & 🌐 Universal
- "한국인의 80%가 알고 있는 비결... 그런데 대부분 틀렸다." (The secret 80% know... but get wrong) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A2 (Primary), A3 (Secondary), A5-A7 (Product narrative), C1-C3 (Hero/narrative), D2 (Emotional hook)

---

### Concrete

**Definition:** Replace abstractions with sensory details. Instead of "high quality," show *how* it's built. Instead of "fast growth," cite numbers and real milestones. Make ideas visible and tangible.

**Copy Application Rule:** Use numbers, not vague claims. Describe what users will *do* or *see*, not what they'll *feel*. Show the mechanism. Use verbs of action. Include specific details: names, dates, measurements, before/after states.

**Examples:**
- "Saves 8 hours per week" (concrete number) vs. "Saves significant time" (vague) 🌐 Universal
- "3,200 creators earned $10K+ last month" (specific achievement) not "thousands of creators prosper" 🎨 C8
- "휴대폰을 꺼내는 2초 만에 사진이 저장된다" (Saved in 2 seconds from phone) vs. "빠르게 저장됨" (Saves quickly) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A1 (Numbers in headline), A2 (Primary benefit), A3 (Secondary), A4 (Detail copy), B-type (Data-driven copy), C2-C4 (Product detail)

---

### Credible

**Definition:** Establish authority through evidence, not assertion. Use real data, expert validation, customer proof, or transparent methodology instead of marketing claims.

**Copy Application Rule:** Show, don't claim. Use third-party validation (awards, reviews, independent data). Reveal the "seams"—explain how it works. Cite your source when using stats. Include the "trustworthiness signal": small details that prove you did the research.

**Examples:**
- "Recommended by 47 published sleep scientists" (3rd-party credibility) vs. "Scientists recommend us" 🌐 Universal
- "We charge 20% more than competitors, and here's why..." (Transparency builds trust) 🎨 C8 & 🌐 Universal
- "한국 소비자원 만족도 1위" (Official award) not "소비자들의 신뢰를 받고 있습니다" (vague trust claim) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A2 (Primary), A4 (Detail/credibility), B2-B3 (Trust-focused universal copy), C1-C2 (Hero credibility)

---

### Emotional

**Definition:** Connect to core human values and aspirations. Emotion drives decisions, even for "rational" purchases. The best copy makes people *feel* something before they think about buying.

**Copy Application Rule:** Identify the core fear or desire beneath the product. Write to that emotion, not the feature. Use specific, vivid language that triggers emotion. Show transformation, not transaction. Appeal to identity and belonging, not vanity.

**Examples:**
- "Stop wasting your evenings. Reclaim your family time." (Fear of lost time, desire for connection) 🌐 Universal
- "You'll be the one in the room who actually knows what's happening." (Identity/status, but authentic) 🎨 C8
- "당신의 엄마가 자랑할 수 있는 일을 하세요" (Do work your mom would be proud of) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A3 (Secondary/emotional), A5-A7 (Narrative/story), C1 (Hero/emotional), D3 (Testimonial/emotional proof)

---

### Stories

**Definition:** Narratives stick in memory better than facts. A story creates a sequence of events, characters, conflict, and resolution that naturally encode information.

**Copy Application Rule:** Structure as Problem → Struggle → Solution → Transformation. Include a protagonist (customer, not brand). Show resistance overcome, not just benefits received. Use dialogue and sensory detail. Keep it short (3-5 sentences for web copy).

**Examples:**
- "Sarah spent 90 minutes every morning on routine tasks. Then she automated 7 workflows. Now she leaves at 4pm." (Problem → Action → Outcome) 🌐 Universal
- "한 개발자가 프레임워크 없이 앱을 4개월에 만들었습니다. 이 도구를 썼으면 2주였겠죠." (Developer's struggle, then quick win with tool) 🎨 C8
- "Before: Hours in spreadsheets. After: One click, done." (Implicit story in contrast) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A5-A7 (Narrative-heavy), C1-C4 (Story-driven), D2-D3 (Customer story/testimonial)

## PART 2: STEPPS (Contagious)

### Social Currency

**Definition:** People share things that make them look good. Content that gives them status, insider knowledge, or a way to appear smart/creative is more likely to spread.

**Copy Application Rule:** Give people something *worth* sharing. Make them feel like insiders. Provide useful information they can pass along. Create a sense of exclusive access. Avoid obvious self-promotion; frame it as "insight" or "useful data."

**Examples:**
- "The 5 mistakes 90% of founders make (and how to avoid them)" — shareable insight, makes sharer look smart 🌐 Universal
- "우리 팀이 분석한 올해 트렌드 탑 10" (Our team's trend analysis) — insider knowledge 🎨 C8
- "이 한 가지를 바꾸면 당신의 팀도 5배 빨라질 겁니다" (Shareable pro tip) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A3-A4 (Educational/insight), B4-B5 (Thought leadership), C2-C4 (Creator tips), D3 (Social proof that's worth sharing)

---

### Triggers

**Definition:** Ideas spread when cues in the environment remind people of them. Ties your message to frequent thoughts or stimuli in your audience's world.

**Copy Application Rule:** Connect your product to something your audience thinks about *daily*. Use seasonal/contextual references. Build associations with existing habits. Use words and metaphors tied to their environment.

**Examples:**
- Morning routine product: "Your coffee's brewing. So is your focus." (Links to daily morning thought) 🌐 Universal
- "월요일 아침 기분 좋아지는 도구" (Monday morning tool — weekly trigger) 🌐 Universal
- Email for creators: "Your muse takes weekends off. This doesn't." (Tied to creative struggle they face) 🎨 C8

**Best for:** A2-A3 (Frequent-use products), B3-B4 (Habitual touchpoints), C2-C3 (Creator tools tied to workflow), D1-D2 (Awareness/reminder)

---

### Emotion

**Definition:** High-arousal emotions (awe, excitement, anger, anxiety) increase sharing. Low-arousal emotions (sadness) don't. People share content that moves them emotionally, not content that depresses them.

**Copy Application Rule:** Aim for high-arousal positive emotion (awe, inspiration, delight) or negative but actionable emotion (urgency, slight fear). Make people feel something *strong* enough to act. Avoid sad or deflating emotion.

**Examples:**
- "We rebuilt payments. You're welcome." (Delight, ownership) 🎨 C8 & 🌐 Universal
- "This library saves 200 hours per year. Imagine what you'll build." (Awe + agency) 🎨 C8
- "스타트업이 1년에 실패하는 이유 5가지 (+ 승리한 방법)" (Useful anxiety + hope) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A3-A5 (Emotional resonance), C1-C3 (Inspiring/delighting creators), D2 (Emotionally driven action)

---

### Public

**Definition:** Ideas are more likely to spread when behavior is visible to others. If people can see others using/adopting it, they're more likely to adopt it too.

**Copy Application Rule:** Make adoption visible. Show social proof early. Highlight how many people use it. Create shareable moments (badges, achievements, public wins). Use "joining a movement" language.

**Examples:**
- "Joined by 50K creators this month" (Visible adoption) 🎨 C8 & 🌐 Universal
- "See who's using this in your industry" (Peer visibility) 🌐 Universal
- "당신의 친구 3명이 이미 시작했습니다" (Friends-already-using visibility) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** B2-B4 (Social proof-heavy), C2-C4 (Creator network visibility), D1-D2 (Awareness/joining trend)

---

### Practical Value

**Definition:** People share useful information. Tips, hacks, cost-saving advice, and productivity insights spread because they help the recipient.

**Copy Application Rule:** Lead with usefulness. Ask: "Would my audience share this with their peer/manager/friend?" Include actionable steps. Give away real value, not just teasers. Make the benefit obvious immediately.

**Examples:**
- "How to cut your cloud bill 40% (and keep performance)" (Useful, saves money) 🌐 Universal
- "3줄 이메일로 거절하는 방법" (Practical tip, easy to execute) 🌐 Universal
- "오픈소스 라이센스 5분 가이드" (Quick, practical resource) 🎨 C8 & 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A4 (Detail/tips), B3-B5 (Educational/how-to), C2-C4 (Creator tutorials/tips), D3 (Helpful testimonial)

---

### Stories (STEPPS)

**Definition:** Stories are the most viral vehicle for information. They embed practical value inside a narrative that people want to tell others.

**Copy Application Rule:** Wrap practical tips inside a narrative. Show transformation through character action, not abstract benefit. Use the "before/after" structure but make it feel like storytelling, not sales pitch.

**Examples:**
- "A designer wasted 15 hours weekly on asset management. He found this tool and now spends 2 hours. Here's his system." (Story + practical tip) 🎨 C8
- "큰 프로젝트로 번아웃된 개발자... 그리고 60%의 시간 절약" (Story of transformation) 🎨 C8
- "From scattered notes to published book in 6 months. Here's how." (Story framework for practical journey) 🌐 Universal

**Best for:** A5-A7 (Narrative-primary), C1-C4 (Creator stories), D2-D3 (Social proof/case study format)

## Combined Checklist: Sticky + Shareable Copy

Before you ship, score yourself on this 0-10 scale:

### Stickiness (SUCCESs)
- [ ] **Simple** (10): Can someone repeat your core message in one sentence?
- [ ] **Unexpected** (10): Does it violate or surprise an expectation?
- [ ] **Concrete** (10): Are there specific numbers, names, or actions visible?
- [ ] **Credible** (10): Is there evidence, data, or third-party validation?
- [ ] **Emotional** (10): Does it appeal to fear, desire, identity, or belonging?
- [ ] **Stories** (10): Is there a protagonist, struggle, and transformation?

### Shareability (STEPPS)
- [ ] **Social Currency** (10): Would sharing this make the reader look smart/inside?
- [ ] **Triggers** (10): Does it connect to daily thoughts or frequent contexts?
- [ ] **Emotion** (10): Is the emotion high-arousal (awe, excitement, urgency)?
- [ ] **Public** (10): Is adoption/usage visible to others?
- [ ] **Practical Value** (10): Would readers want to pass this to peers?
- [ ] **Stories** (10): Is it wrapped in a narrative they'll want to retell?

**Scoring Guide:**
- 60+: Ready to publish. Likely to stick and spread.
- 45-59: Good; consider strengthening 2-3 weakest areas.
- Below 45: Major revision needed. Focus on adding story + emotion.

## Framework Mapping to Copy Types

**A-Types (Product Marketing):**
- A1 (Hero/Headline): Simple + Concrete + Unexpected. Social Currency for shareability.
- A2 (Primary): Credible + Emotional. Add Triggers for retention.
- A3 (Secondary): Emotional + Stories. Practical Value for shareability.
- A4 (Detail/Education): Concrete + Practical Value. Add Triggers.
- A5-A7 (Narrative/Case): Stories + Emotional. High STEPPS overall.

**B-Types (Universal):**
- B1-B2 (Brand): Simple + Credible + Stories.
- B3-B5 (Thought Leadership): Concrete + Practical Value + Social Currency.

**C-Types (Creator/Product):**
- C8 Mode (Hip/Creator): Unexpected + Emotional + Stories. All STEPPS for virality.
- C1 (Hero): Simple + Unexpected + Emotional + Stories.
- C2-C4 (Product): Concrete + Practical Value. Triggers for workflow integration.

**D-Types (Campaign):**
- D1 (Awareness): Unexpected + Triggers. Strong Social Currency.
- D2 (Engagement): Emotional + Stories. Practical Value.
- D3 (Conversion/Testimonial): Credible + Stories. Emotional proof.
