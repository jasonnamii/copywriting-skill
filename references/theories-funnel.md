# Funnel Frameworks: AIDA + PAS + Awareness Levels + Stone's 7-Step

## PART 1: AIDA (Classic Conversion Funnel)

**Framework Definition:** The four sequential stages of customer decision-making. Each stage requires specific copy moves to progress the prospect deeper.

### Stage 1: Attention

**Definition:** Get noticed. Cut through noise. Trigger curiosity or recognition.

**Copy Writing Rules:**
- Headline must be specific, not generic. Use numbers, bold claims, or questions.
- Lead with a problem the audience recognizes *immediately*.
- Use contrasts, reversals, or surprising data.
- Keep opening sentence to 10 words or fewer.
- Use sensory language or pattern-breaks.

**Diagnosis:** Audience has no awareness. They don't know the problem exists or that your solution is possible.

**Examples:**
- "Your code is 60% slower than it could be." (Specific problem recognition) 🌐 Universal
- "한국 개발자 70%가 모르는 성능 최적화" (Local insight + gap) 🌐 Universal
- "Designers waste 8 hours/week on repetitive tasks. (We fixed that.)" (Problem + promise) 🎨 C8

**Best Copy Types:** A1 (Hero), D1 (Awareness), A2 (Initial hook)

---

### Stage 2: Interest

**Definition:** Make them want to learn more. Show that a solution exists and why it matters to *them*.

**Copy Writing Rules:**
- Explain the problem's cost (time, money, opportunity lost).
- Show a glimpse of the solution without overselling.
- Move from "What?" to "Why should I care?"
- Use story openings to deepen engagement.
- Answer the unspoken question: "Is this worth my time?"

**Diagnosis:** Audience recognizes the problem but isn't sure a solution exists or that it's worth exploring.

**Examples:**
- "Most development teams lose 15+ hours/week coordinating code reviews. There's a better way." (Cost + hope) 🌐 Universal
- "당신이 야근하는 이유는 도구가 없어서다. (아니면 그럴 수도 있다.)" (Reframes cause, builds curiosity) 🎨 C8
- "The #1 mistake founders make is underestimating how long onboarding takes." (Authority + interest) 🌐 Universal

**Best Copy Types:** A2 (Primary), A3 (Secondary), D2 (Engagement)

---

### Stage 3: Desire

**Definition:** Make them *want* it specifically. Move from "a solution exists" to "this solution is right for me."

**Copy Writing Rules:**
- Show transformation, not features. "Your morning changes" not "5-minute setup."
- Include specific outcomes (numbers, timelines).
- Add emotional resonance. Show how life improves.
- Use social proof. Others like them are using it.
- Create slight urgency or scarcity (legitimately).

**Diagnosis:** Audience is interested but comparing alternatives or doubting if it's worth the investment.

**Examples:**
- "You'll ship features 3x faster. Plus, your whole team will actually understand the code." (Outcome + emotional benefit) 🌐 Universal
- "3,000 creators shipped their first product last month. You could be next." (Social proof + possibility) 🎨 C8
- "엄마는 더 이상 당신 핸드폰을 시간낭비 도구라고 안 볼 겁니다" (Identity transformation) 🌐 Universal

**Best Copy Types:** A3 (Secondary), A5-A7 (Narrative), C1 (Hero/transformation)

---

### Stage 4: Action

**Definition:** Remove friction. Make the next step obvious and easy.

**Copy Writing Rules:**
- Use active verbs. "Start your free trial" not "Begin your onboarding journey."
- Make the CTA obvious and single-minded.
- Remove doubt with a safety guarantee or trial period.
- Answer: "What happens after I click?"
- Use urgency only if genuine (limited slots, deadline, pricing change).

**Diagnosis:** Audience wants it but needs permission to move. Friction: price, commitment, unclear what happens next.

**Examples:**
- "Start free. No credit card required. Runs in 5 minutes." (Remove all friction) 🌐 Universal
- "지금 시작하면 30일 무료. 언제든지 취소 가능." (Guarantee + permission) 🌐 Universal
- "See your first result in 10 minutes. Then decide." (Lower commitment barrier) 🎨 C8

**Best Copy Types:** A1 (CTA in hero), A4 (Detail + guarantees), D3 (Conversion driver)

## PART 2: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

**Framework Definition:** A persuasion structure that deepens the audience's awareness of pain before presenting the solution.

### Stage 1: Problem

**Definition:** Clearly state the problem in the customer's language, not your language.

**Copy Writing Rules:**
- Use their words, not industry jargon.
- Be specific. "Slow loading" not "performance degradation."
- Acknowledge the problem is real and common.
- Avoid blame; frame as a structural issue.
- Use the second person: "You face..."

**Diagnosis:** Audience has the problem but may not have named it or prioritized it.

**Examples:**
- "You spend 3 hours a week on tasks a machine could do in 3 minutes." (Specific, relatable) 🌐 Universal
- "당신의 팀은 1시간 회의를 했는데 2시간이 지나갔다." (Relatable frustration) 🌐 Universal
- "Your best creators burn out within 18 months because of admin work." (Industry-specific pain) 🎨 C8

**Best Copy Types:** A2 (Primary), A3 (Secondary), D2 (Engagement)

---

### Stage 2: Agitate

**Definition:** Deepen the emotional impact of the problem. Show consequences, compound costs, missed opportunities.

**Copy Writing Rules:**
- Expand on the problem's ripple effects.
- Show time cost + money cost + emotional cost.
- Ask "What if" questions that make discomfort obvious.
- Avoid being manipulative; stay truthful about consequences.
- Make it specific to their situation (e.g., "creators" not "people").

**Diagnosis:** Audience feels the problem at surface level but hasn't calculated its real cost.

**Examples:**
- "That's 150 hours a year wasted. Or $45K in lost productivity. Or 50 nights at home you didn't get back." (Multiple angles of cost) 🌐 Universal
- "3시간 일주일이 아니라... 년간 150시간이 사라진다. 그 사이에 몇 제품을 만들 수 있을까?" (Reframe to larger impact) 🎨 C8
- "While you're managing permissions, your competitor is shipping." (Opportunity cost) 🌐 Universal

**Best Copy Types:** A3 (Secondary), D2 (Engagement)

---

### Stage 3: Solve

**Definition:** Present the solution as the obvious answer to the problem you've articulated.

**Copy Writing Rules:**
- Make the connection explicit: "That's why we built X."
- Lead with the mechanism, not the name.
- Show how it addresses the specific agitation you raised.
- Use the same language you used in Problem/Agitate.
- Make it believable (show how/why it works).

**Diagnosis:** You've made them feel pain; now show them the path out.

**Examples:**
- "We automated those 3 hours. Now your team ships 3x faster and goes home on time." (Direct answer to agitation) 🌐 Universal
- "우리는 그 150시간을 10시간으로 줄였다. 대신 140시간으로 새 제품을 만들어라." (Solution quantified in same terms as agitation) 🎨 C8
- "You manage code in the same interface where you ship it. Permissions take 30 seconds." (Mechanism-based solution) 🌐 Universal

**Best Copy Types:** A4 (Detail), A5-A7 (Narrative), C1 (Hero)

## PART 3: Five Levels of Awareness (Schwartz)

**Framework Definition:** The prospect's starting awareness level determines what copy will resonate. Wrong messaging for their awareness level = low conversion.

### Level 1: Unaware

**Definition:** Audience doesn't know the problem exists.

**Copy Application:**
- Headline must educate, not sell.
- Start with an unusual fact or observation.
- Use the Problem-Agitate-Solve structure fully.
- Introduce the *category* before the solution.
- Expect lower immediate conversion; aim for awareness first.

**Diagnosis:** Ask: "Do they know this problem matters?" If no, they're unaware.

**Examples:**
- "Your team is losing 3 hours a week to a problem you don't track." (Introduces problem) 🌐 Universal
- "당신이 알지 못하는 성능 누수가 있습니다." (Introduces hidden problem) 🌐 Universal
- "Most creators don't realize how much time they waste on format conversion." (Unaware → Aware) 🎨 C8

---

### Level 2: Problem-Aware

**Definition:** Audience knows the problem exists but may not know solutions are available.

**Copy Application:**
- Headline can acknowledge the problem directly.
- Show that solutions *exist* without overselling yours.
- Use stories of others solving it.
- Focus on credibility and proof, not novelty.
- Expect moderate-to-high conversion.

**Diagnosis:** Ask: "Do they know the problem has solutions?" If yes but hesitant, they're problem-aware.

**Examples:**
- "Everyone knows code reviews are slow. Here's how teams cut review time 60%." (Problem acknowledged, solutions exist) 🌐 Universal
- "디버깅은 항상 오래 걸린다고 생각했습니다. 지금 생각이 바뀔 겁니다." (Problem known, solution surprising) 🌐 Universal
- "You know admin work kills productivity. See how teams eliminated it." (Problem-aware, showing solution path) 🎨 C8

---

### Level 3: Solution-Aware

**Definition:** They know solutions exist but are comparing options and might not know yours.

**Copy Application:**
- Lead with differentiation, not problem explanation.
- Use competitive comparison (implicit or explicit).
- Lead with unique mechanism or proof.
- Show why *this* solution beats alternatives.
- Expect high conversion if copy is strong.

**Diagnosis:** Ask: "Are they shopping competitors?" If yes, they're solution-aware.

**Examples:**
- "Most code review tools prioritize speed. We prioritize team growth. Here's the difference." (Differentiated positioning) 🌐 Universal
- "기존 도구는 빠르지만 복잡하다. 우리는 빠르면서도 쉽다." (Competitive differentiation) 🎨 C8
- "Where we differ: we're the only platform where reviewers learn something every time." (Unique value) 🌐 Universal

---

### Level 4: Product-Aware

**Definition:** They know your product exists and are deciding whether to buy.

**Copy Application:**
- Lead with outcome or guarantee.
- Remove all friction and doubt.
- Use strongest social proof and credibility.
- Make it easy to say yes (trial, guarantee, ease).
- Expect very high conversion with right messaging.

**Diagnosis:** They've visited your page before or know your brand.

**Examples:**
- "Used by 8K teams shipping faster code. 30-day money-back guarantee." (Proof + permission) 🌐 Universal
- "무료로 시작. 신용카드 불필요. 언제든 환불." (Guarantee + ease) 🌐 Universal
- "Your team will save 2 hours/week or your money back." (Outcome guarantee) 🎨 C8

---

### Level 5: Most-Aware

**Definition:** They understand your solution and are deciding between you and a close alternative.

**Copy Application:**
- Lead with micro-differentiation (nuance, implementation, support).
- Use specific, proven outcomes (numbers only).
- Remove any remaining doubt with guarantees or trial.
- Make the decision easy (comparison, side-by-side).
- Expect highest conversion with minimal copy.

**Diagnosis:** They're in final stages of decision. Copy should be minimal and decision-focused.

**Examples:**
- "Ships 40% faster than [competitor]. Used by [customers they know]. 14-day setup guarantee." (Direct comparison) 🌐 Universal
- "우리는 한국 팀을 위해 만들어졌습니다. 지원도 한글입니다." (Micro-differentiation for specific market) 🌐 Universal
- "Same price, but your whole team learns. Here's proof." (Outcome differentiation) 🎨 C8

## PART 4: Stone's 7-Step Copy Formula

**Framework Definition:** A hybrid structure combining emotional resonance with logical progression.

### Step 1: Get Attention

**Definition:** Hook with a surprising question, fact, or problem statement.

**Copy Rule:** Lead with something unexpected or immediately relevant to their situation.

**Example:** "Your product is great. Your onboarding is killing it." (Contradiction surprise)

---

### Step 2: Identify the Problem

**Definition:** Name the specific problem your audience faces.

**Copy Rule:** Use their language. Be specific. Show you understand their world.

**Example:** "Teams spend 30% of dev time in meetings, not building." (Industry-specific problem)

---

### Step 3: Paint the Picture

**Definition:** Show the emotional cost or consequence of the problem.

**Copy Rule:** Use sensory detail and future pacing. "Imagine if..."

**Example:** "Your best engineers are frustrated. Your roadmap is behind. Your team is burned out." (Emotional consequences)

---

### Step 4: Position the Solution

**Definition:** Introduce your solution as the bridge from problem to transformation.

**Copy Rule:** Make it seem inevitable, not promotional. "That's why we built..."

**Example:** "We built the only platform where code and collaboration live in one place." (Solution introduction)

---

### Step 5: Support with Proof

**Definition:** Validate your solution with evidence, data, or stories.

**Copy Rule:** Use the strongest proof available. Third-party > customer > internal.

**Example:** "8,000 teams save an average of 12 hours/week. Here's their story." (Quantified + narrative)

---

### Step 6: Overcome Objections

**Definition:** Address the most likely hesitations before they're asked.

**Copy Rule:** Anticipate the "but what about...?" questions.

**Example:** "Your security is our priority. We're SOC2, encrypted end-to-end, audited annually." (Common objection answered)

---

### Step 7: Call to Action

**Definition:** Make the next step obvious and easy.

**Copy Rule:** Single, clear action. Remove friction.

**Example:** "Start your free trial. 5-minute setup, no credit card required." (Easy, low-commitment)

---

## Awareness Stage Diagnosis Decision Tree

```
Does the audience know a problem exists?
├─ NO → Use 5-Level awareness positioning (Unaware)
│   ├─ Start with education-focused headline
│   ├─ Use PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve) structure fully
│   └─ Expect 15-30% engagement to next step
│
├─ YES → Do they know solutions exist?
│   ├─ NO → Problem-Aware positioning
│   │   ├─ Acknowledge problem directly
│   │   ├─ Show solutions are emerging
│   │   ├─ Use AIDA Interest/Desire stages
│   │   └─ Expect 30-50% engagement
│   │
│   └─ YES → Do they know YOUR solution?
│       ├─ NO → Solution-Aware positioning
│       │   ├─ Lead with differentiation
│       │   ├─ Use competitive contrast
│       │   ├─ Use AIDA Desire stage
│       │   └─ Expect 50-70% engagement
│       │
│       └─ YES → Are they deciding between you and others?
│           ├─ NO → Product-Aware positioning
│           │   ├─ Lead with guarantee/trial
│           │   ├─ Maximum proof focus
│           │   ├─ Use AIDA Action stage
│           │   └─ Expect 70-85% engagement
│           │
│           └─ YES → Most-Aware positioning
│               ├─ Lead with micro-differentiation
│               ├─ Minimal copy, maximum clarity
│               ├─ Use specific outcome comparison
│               └─ Expect 80-95% engagement
```

## Framework Selection by Copy Type

**A-Types (Product):**
- A1 (Hero): AIDA Attention + 5-Levels framework for diagnosis
- A2 (Primary): AIDA Attention/Interest, PAS Problem/Agitate
- A3 (Secondary): AIDA Interest/Desire, PAS Solve
- A4 (Detail): Stone's 7-Step (Support/Overcome)
- A5-A7 (Narrative): AIDA full funnel + Stone's 7-Step structure

**B-Types (Universal):**
- B1-B5: 5-Levels awareness positioning + AIDA framework as backbone

**C-Types (Creator):**
- C1 (Hero): 5-Levels diagnosis + AIDA/Stone's 7-Step
- C2-C4 (Product): PAS structure + AIDA Desire/Action
- C8 Mode: Emphasize emotional stages (AIDA Interest, Stone's Paint Picture step)

**D-Types (Campaign):**
- D1 (Awareness): 5-Levels Unaware positioning + AIDA Attention
- D2 (Engagement): PAS full structure
- D3 (Conversion): AIDA full funnel + Stone's 7-Step
